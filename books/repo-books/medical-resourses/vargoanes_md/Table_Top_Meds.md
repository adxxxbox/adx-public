**Succinylcholine-**3cc syringe (2mg/kg) higher dose  than adults because peds have a higher volume distribution (Extra Cellular space). They are more prone to cardiac Dysrhythmias , myoglomemia, increased K,  Malignant Hyperthermia and masseter spasm.Always have small needle attached to it incase of emergency IM injection. 22G or 20H is easier than an 18G

**Have 1 of the 3 muscle relaxants below-**(Duration of some nondeplorizers can last 2x longer because of their immature liver)

**Atracrium-**0.5mg/kg 1 or 3 cc syringe

**Vecuronium-**0.1mg/kg 1-3 cc syrince

**Rocoronium-**0.6-1.2mg/kg 1-3 cc syringe

**Atropine-**0.02mg/kg dilute in a 5 or 10cc syringe (Atropine should be co-administered or proceeded with Succinylcholine because of risk of bradycardia or asystole.)(03mg/kg-reflex brady from Succinylcholine )

**Propofol-** 3mg/kg in a 3 or 10cc syringe

**Have 1 or 2 of the Common Narcotics below:**

**Fentanyl-**Dose- 1-2 mcg/kg (add 1cc to 4cc of NS in a 5cc syringe=10mcg/cc) or (1cc to 9cc in 10cc syringe = 5mcg/cc)

**Morphine-**Dose 0.1mg/kg-get 10mg vial, mix in 9cc=1mg/cc)

**Flush syringes** with NS, LR or Albumin

**Endotracheal Tubes and stylet –**have 3, the appropriate size and ½ size larger and smaller available

For predicted uncuffed OETT size= age / 4 +4 Predicted Size Cuffed OETT  size  = (Age / 4) + 3 also 16 + Age (years) / 4 = ETT size

Cuffed endotubes- subtract 0.5 if kid < 10kg ex uncuffed 4.0mm, so cuffed 3.5mm subtract 1.0 if kid > 10kg
