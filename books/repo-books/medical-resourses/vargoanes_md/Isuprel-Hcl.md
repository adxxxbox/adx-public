# Isuprel HCl

**Isuprel Hcl (Isoproterenol)**  
**Class:** Antiarrhythmic  
Sympathomimetic, inotrope and chronotrope.  
(B1/B2) agonist-the most potent combined.  
**Doses:**  
**IV infusion:** 1-20mcg/ min  
**Usual initial rate:** 5mcg/min  
Titrate to HR and BP.  
**Peds:** initially 0.02-0.1 mcg/kg/min followed by 0.05-2mcg/kg/min.  
Rates over 30 mcg per minute have been used in advanced stages of shock.  
Adjust doses based of HR, CVP, BP, and UO. If the heart rate exceeds 110 bpm, decrease IV drip rate.  
Add 2mg of Isuprel to 500mL of D5 or NS = 4mcg/mL.  
Or add 1mg to a 250mL bag =4mcg/mL.  
**Analog of epinephrine.**  
Beta-sympathomimetic, selective Beta 1, but has some Beta 2 that acts on the heart, bronchial smooth muscle skeletal muscle and alimentary tract.  
Lowers PVR-mostly in skeletal muscle and renal vascular beds.  
**Increases:** BP, HR, CO/CI and PAP  
**Decreases:** SVR, CVP and PVR  
**Metabolized:** by liver and COMP.  
Can cause hyperglycemia  
**Indications:** For the treatment of mild or transient episodes of heart block that do not require electric shock or pacemaker therapy also used in management of refractory asthma because it is a great bronchodilator.  
  
**Contraindications:** Patients with known hypersensitivity, with tachyarrhythmias; tachycardia or heart block caused by digitalis intoxication; ventricular arrhythmias which require inotropic therapy; and angina pectoris.  
**Adverse Reactions:** CNS-nervousness, headache, dizziness.  
**Cardiovascular:** Tachycardia, palpitations, angina, Adams-Stokes attacks, pulmonary edema, hypertension, hypotension, ventricular arrhythmias, tachyarrhythmias.  
**Also:** Flushing of the skin, sweating, mild tremors, weakness, nausea and vomiting.
