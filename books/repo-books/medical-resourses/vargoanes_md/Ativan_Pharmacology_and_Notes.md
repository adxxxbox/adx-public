# Ativan (Lorazapam) Pharmacology and Notes

Ativan is starting to be replaced in the ICUs with low dose propofol infusions because propofol does not drop your patients pressure as much as Ativan and the patients wake up quicker.  
Both drugs are amnestic  

**Class**_:_

benzodiazepine

* * *

**Dosing Frequency**_:_

Q4-8 hours  

* * *

**Indications**:

Management of anxiety and status epilepticus. Sedative for patients who will need prolonged sedation.

* * *

**Mechanism**:

Depresses CNS through increased action of GABA.  
(GABA) gamma aminobutyric acid: an amino acid that is found in the central nervous system; acts as an inhibitory neurotransmitter

Amnestic drug like versed  

* * *

**Metabolism**:

Liver  

* * *

**Elimination**:

Urinary excretion  

* * *

**Half Life:**

13-16 hours, (40 hours in neonates), ESRD 32-70 hours  

* * *

**Adverse Effects**:  
resp depression  
tachycardia  
drowsiness  
confusion  
diaphoresis  
paradoxal excitment  
burns

Ativan is made with propylene glycol as an excipient. Unfortunately, it burns with administration. You should dilute it for IVP.
