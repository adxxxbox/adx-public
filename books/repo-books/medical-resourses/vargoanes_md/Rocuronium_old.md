# Rocuronium

**Rocuronium (Zemuron) (Roc)**  
**Class:** NDMD (Intermediate Acting)  
**Subclass:** Monoquaternary aminosteroid.  
**Structure:** Resembles Vecuronium except for the presence of a hydroxyl group rather than an acetyl group on the A-ring of the steroid nucleus.  
#1 muscle relaxer we get burned redsosing and have to wait extra long for the return of twitches. Be careful redosing.  
Muscle relaxer-reversible competitive antagonism of Ach  
**Doses**  
**ED95:** 0.3mg/kg  
**Intubating dose:** 0.6-1.2mg/kg  
**RSI dose:**1-1.2mg/kg  
**Redose:** 0.1mg/kg  
**Peds (IM):** 1-1.8mg/kg  
**Drip:** 3-12mcg/kg/min  
**Onset:** 1-2 minutes  
**Peak:** 60-90 seconds  
**Duration:** 20-35 minutes  
**Renal Excretion:** 30%  
**Biliary Excretion (liver) -70%**  
**Histamine Release:** Yes  
**Heart Rate Change:** minimal; increased in Peds.  
**Malignant Hyperthemia Trigger:** No  
Not as potent as a Vecuronium.  
**Rapid onset:** The alternate drug to Sucs for an RSI at large doses.  
At large doses it resembles Sucs at the adductor pollicis but the onset is still a little slower.  
As with other nondepolarizing drugs the laryngeal adductor muscles and diaphragm are more resistant than the adductor pollicis. So a single twitch response of the adductor pollicis does not mean paralysis at the diaphragm and laryngeal muscles.  
More anesthesia providers will say they have been burned more waiting for twitch returns at the end of cases as compared to Norcuron.  
I have seen some anesthesia providers redose with Norcuron because they are both aminosteroids.  
Ephedrine shortens the onset  
**Factors that INCREASE the block of NDMDs**  
**(Increase sensitive, Prolong the duration)**  
Atypical Pseudocholinesterase  
Volatile Anesthetics, least with Nitrous Oxide  
Aminoglycoside antibiotics  
Cardiac antidysrhythmic drugs  
Diuretics-lasix at 1mg/kg will  
Local Anesthetics: Amides  
HYPOkalemia and HYPERmagnesemia  
Respiratory Acidosis  
Hypothermia-mild hypothermia can double the duration  
Calcium Channel Blockers  
Lithium  
Immunosuppressants  
Renal and Hepatic disease  
Neuromuscular diseases  
Antineoplastics  
**Factors that shorten the duration of NDMDs**  
**Decreases sensitivity (shortens the duration)**  
Burns- between 10-60 days. Must be >30% burn  
Massive Trauma  
Prolonged Immobility  
Aminophylline  
Steroids  
Dilantin  
CVA
