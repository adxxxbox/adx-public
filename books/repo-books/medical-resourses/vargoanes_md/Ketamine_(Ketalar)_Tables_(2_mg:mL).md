# Ketamine (Ketalar) 2mg/mL  
Ketamine Drip

We rarely run a Ketamine drip in the anesthesia world today. We usually just add it to a propofol syringe (drip) or give it alone in small doses during certain procedures involving chronic pain patients.  
**Chronic Pain patients** 0.5-1.0 mg/kg bolus just prior to incision and then bolus around 10mg every hour after.  
**As an adjuvant to both Propofol and Opioids**  
Many dose discrepancies between literatures.  
0.5mg/kg bolus prior to incision, then infuse  
0.1-0.3mg/kg/hr.  
Another popular dose is 10-50mcg/kg/min (after initial bolus).  

**Tip:**  
It is safe to keep total HOURLY maintenance dose < 0.2mg/kg and discontinue 45-60 minutes before emergence.

**TIVA**  
White Lightning(ketofol)-mix 50mg Ketamine in 50mL of Propofol and infuse with syringe pump.

* * *

**For 2mg/mL**  
Dilute 100mg of Ketamine to a 60mL syringe with NS to get 50mL total.  
This = 2mg/mL **Example:**  
Say you want 0.2mg/kg/hr on a 70kg patient  
0.2 x 70 / 2 = 7  
so 0.2mg/kg/hr= 7ml/hr
