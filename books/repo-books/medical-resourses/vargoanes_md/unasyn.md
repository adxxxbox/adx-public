# Unasyn

**Unasyn (Ampicillin and Sulbactam)**  
**Class:** Penicillin combination  
**Doses:**  
1.5-3 grams Give over 15-30 mins  
**Common adult dose:** 3 grams IV  
**Max:** 12g/day  
**Peds**  
**Infants >1 month:** 100-300mg (ampicillin component)/kg/day IV in divided doses Q6h.  
**Children>1 year:** 100-400mg (ampicillin component)/kg/day IV in divided doses Q6H  
**PEDS Max:** 8 grams ampicillin/day  
**Indications:**  
Intra-Abdominal and Gynecological Infections.  
Acinetobacter and coccobacillary bacteria that cause several hospital acquired infections-pneumonia and wound infections.  
To treat infections caused by bacteria resistant to beta-lactam antibiotics.  
**MOA**  
Sulbactam (beta-lactamase inhibitor) blocks the enzyme which breaks down ampicillin and thereby allows ampicillin to attack and kill the bacteria.  
Ampicillin also acts as a competitive inhibitor of the enzyme transpeptidase, which is needed by bacteria to make their cell walls. It inhibits the third and final stage of bacterial cell wall synthesis in binary fission, which ultimately leads to cell lysis.  
**Administration:** IV or IM  
**Contraindications:**  
Allergy to Ampicillin and Sulbactum.  
Patient is taking tetracycline antibiotic (eg, doxycycline).  
Mononucleosis  
Patient has recently received or will be receiving live oral typhoid vaccine.
