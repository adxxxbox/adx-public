### MH How to Prepare for a Patient with MH Susceptibility

_Edited by Dan Lovinaria, DNP, MS, MBA, APRN, CRNA_

Alternatives to Anesthesia Machine Preparation for MH Susceptible patients:  
**Anesthesia Machine Flush  
Goal:** Decrease the gas concentration below 5 ppm  
With either oxygen or air, set the ventilator to 700 ml tidal volume, I:E ratio of 1:2, RR  
of 12, PEEP Off, and fresh gas flow rate with 10-15 lpm.

Flush the ventilator according to manufacturer’s recommendations. Most hospital policies require 20 minutes despite literature of newer ventilators requiring much more time.

**Shut down or remove all vaporizers.**

Disconnect vaporizers from the workstation or place a long piece of surgical tape across the vaporizer dials as a reminder.

**Change the ventilator circuit, reservoir bag and CO2 absorbent.**

Attach a new disposable breathing circuit.  
Replace the carbon dioxide absorbent.  
  
During the case, run your fresh gas flow to at least 10 lpm to avoid “rebound phenomenon” (increased release of residual volatile anesthetic agent when fresh gas flow is reduced after a set period of flushing).  
  
OR  
  
**Use charcoal filters**  
Flush the ventilator for 90 seconds at 10 lpm, then attach the charcoal filters.  
  
OR  
  
**Use a dedicated “vapor free” machine** for MH-susceptible patients. The machine must be regularly maintained and safety-checked.  
  
OR  
  
**Use an ICU ventilator** that has never been exposed to volatile anesthetic agents.

**How long to flush the anesthesia machines  
**Newer anesthesia machines: may require up to 104 minutes.  
Older machines: for 20 minutes  
You can call the manufacturer of the machine for specific instructions on preparation of the machine for an MH patient.

**What are Charcoal Filters? (See Charcoal Filters Link)  
**They obviate the need for purging the anesthesia machine for 20 minutes prior to an MH Susceptible Case.  
These filters are effective in keeping gas concentration below 5 ppm for up to 12 hours with fresh gas flows of at least 3 L/min.  
_(They look like filters that you connect to the inspiratory and expiratory outlets of the anesthesia machine. They go between the ventilator inspiratory/expiratory tubing and the ventilator inspiratory/expiratory ports)  
_  
**How to install them:  
**1\. First, you need to flush the anesthesia machine for 90 seconds before placing  
them on the inspiratory and expiratory ports.  
  
2\. Connect them to the ventilators inspiratory and expiratory ports  
  
3\. Connect the ventilator circuit to the open ends of the charcoal filters  
**Charcoal Filter during an MS Crises  
**These filters remove anesthetic gases and obviate the need for purging the anesthesia machine for 20 minutes. However, the anesthesia machine will still need to be flushed with high fresh gas flows (10 L/min) for 90 seconds prior to placing the activated charcoal filters on both the inspiratory and expiratory ports.

Someone will need to manually ventilate/hyperventilate the patient with an ambu bag (or baby safe device) while purging the anesthesia machine.
