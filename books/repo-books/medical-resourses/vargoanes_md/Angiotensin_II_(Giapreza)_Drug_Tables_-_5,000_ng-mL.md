### Angiotensin II (Giapreza) Drug Tables

**5,000 ng/mL in ng/kg/min****Angiotensin II is converted to mcg for continuous infusion.  
Note:** Most infusion pumps do not have nanogram units.  
  
**Continuous infusion dose:** 20 ng/kg/min (0.02 mcg/kg/min)  
\- It can be titrated every 5 minutes by increments of up to 15 ng/kg/min (0.015 mc/mcg/min)  
\- Do not exceed 80 ng/kg/min (0.08 mcg/kg/min) during the first 3 hours.**Maintenance doses:** Should NOT exceed 40 ng/kg/min (0.04 mcg/kg/min)  
\- It can be titrated every 5 minutes by increments of up to 15 ng/kg/min  
\- Do not exceed 80 ng/kg/min during the first 3 hours.\- Doses as low as 1.25 ng/kg/min may be used.  
\- Once the underlying shock has sufficiently improved, down-titrate every 5 to 15 minutes by increments of up to 15 ng/kg/min based on blood pressure.  
\- For critically ill patients, the usual target MAP is 65 – 75 mmHg.  
  
**Nanograms (ng) to micrograms (mcg):  
**1 million ng = 1 mg (1,000 mcg)10,000 ng/mL bag = .01 mg or 10 mcg/mL  
5,000 ng/mL bag = .005 mg or 5 mcg/mL  
20 ng = 0.02 mcg
