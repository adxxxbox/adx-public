### Tumors and Symptoms

Tumors vary greatly and depend on the brain tumor's size, location and rate of growth. In short, sooner or later the tumor grows large enough and increases the ICP. This is when the symptoms arise.

**Frontal Lobe Tumor: Symptoms include** behavioral and emotional changes, impaired judgment, impaired sense of smell, memory loss, paralysis on one side of the body, reduced mental abilities, and vision loss.

**Parietal Lobe Tumor:** Symptoms found are impaired speech, inability to write, lack of recognition, and spatial disorders.

**Occipital lobe:** Symptoms are usually vision loss in one or both eyes.

**Temporal lobe:** Symptoms include an impaired speech pattern and memory difficulty.

**Brainstem Tumor:** Symptoms are usually as follows:  
Behavioral and emotional changes  
Difficulty speaking and swallowing  
Drowsiness  
Hearing loss  
Muscle weakness on one side of the face  
Muscle weakness on one side of the body  
Uncoordinated gait  
Drooping eyelid  
Double vision  
Vomiting
