# Dilantin

**Dilantin (Phenytoin)**  
**Class:** Anticonvulsant  
**Anesthesia Indications:** Prevent seizure caused by neurosurgery.  
Being replaced by Keppra.  
Shortens duration of non-depolarizing neuromuscular blocking drugs.  
**Doses:**  
Popular prophylaxis dose for adult Crani:1 gram.  
**Neurosurgery prophylaxis:** 100-200mg IV Q4.  
**Usual IV Loading Dose:** 10-15mg/kg IV.  
**Cardiac:** 1.5mg/kg IV Q5 until arrhythmia is resolved.  
**Max:** 10-15mg/kg.  
**MOA:**  
An anti-seizure medication that reduces depolarization among brain cells (motor cortex) and extends to peripheral nerves.  
It stabilizes the sodium and possibly Ca ion channels.  
**DO NOT BOLUS:**  
Can use undiluted and administer by SLOW IV injection at a rate. NOT exceeding 50mg/min.  
Recommended rate for most adults: 40 mg/min.  
**Elderly (rate):** 20 mg/min  
Possible rapid bolus SE: Heart block or Bradycardia  
**Normal Plasma Dilantin Range:** 10 to 20 mcg/mL  
Control of seizures are usually obtained when plasma concentrations are 10-20 mcg/mL.  
Plasma concentration of 8-16mcg/mL is usually sufficient to suppress cardiac dysrhythmia.  
**Antidote:** None  
**Protein bound:** 90% Albumin  
**Metabolized:** Liver.  
**Half-life:** About 22 hours, with a range of 7 to 42 hours  
The half-life is shorter in children and longer in the elderly.  
Given IV, it can reach effective plasma levels within 20 minutes.  
**CNS Reactions: (likely when plasma levels >20mcg/mL)**  
Nystagmus ataxia,  
Slurred speech  
Decreased coordination and mental confusion.  
Dizziness  
Insomnia  
Transient nervousness  
Motor twitchings  
**Drug interactions of Dilantin**  
**Increases Effects:**  
Coumarin anticoagulant drugs .  
Hypotention effects of diuretics and other anti-hypertension medications.  
**Decreases Effects**  
Diabetic Meds  
Neuromuscular Blocking Drugs  
**Other Indications (non anesthesia)**  
Treament for partial and generalized seizures.  
Ventricular arrhythmias (old school).  
Intoxication with cardiac glycosides (Dig).  
Some forms of Meniere's syndrome.  
Trigeminal neuralgia.
