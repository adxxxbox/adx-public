# B O suppository

**B & O Suppository (Belladonna/Opium)**  
It contains 2 medications, Belladonna and Opium.  
Belladonna helps decrease bladder and urethral spasms and more commonly administered in GU/Renal procedures.  
Remember: Scopolamine is a Belladonna.  
Opium is a narcotic medication that helps to relieve pain.  
**2 Doses**  
#15 A: Belladonna extract 16.2 mg and opium 30 mg.  
#16 A: Belladonna extract 16.2 mg and opium 60 mg.  
This product is not recommended for use in children 12 years or younger.  
The usual dose is one suppository rectally once or twice daily PRN (as needed), not to exceed four Supprettes in a 24-hour period.  
**Anesthesia Indications:** Sometimes towards the END of a urology case, the surgeon will ask us to give a B & O Suppository to prevent ureteral or bladder spasms. Most of us pass it on to the circulator since she is down on that end of the patient.  
It works by relaxing the smooth muscle of the urinary tract and relieving pain.  
**SIDE EFFECTS:** Nausea, vomiting, constipation, blurred vision, increased eye discomfort in bright light, lightheadedness, dizziness, drowsiness, increased/decreased sweating, or dry mouth may occur.  
**Contraindications:** allergic to narcotics or Scopolamine.  
Glaucoma  
Severe liver or kidney disease  
Seizures  
Severe bleeding  
Hx of head injury  
**Avoid if the patient is on the following medications:**  
linezolid (Zyvox),  
pramlintide (Symlin),  
procarbazine (Matulane),  
naltrexone (ReVia),  
or potassium supplement tablets or capsules.
