### Umbilical Hernia Repair

A protrusion of the intestine and omentum through a hernia in the abdominal wall near the navel; usually self-correcting after birth.

**Anesthetic:**  
**MAC:** For smaller hernias without mesh. Sedation and some Propofol is administered prior to local injection.  MAC is rare for this procedure.  
**General/ETT:** Usually  
**Laparoscopic:** Sometimes.

Mesh is usually used.

**Duration:** 20-40 minutes  
**Position:** Supine with one or both arms out.  
**EBL:** Minimal  
**IV Access:** 20G is acceptable.

**Anti-emetics:** Yes, avoid PONV and protect the new incision.  
**Emergence:** Smooth and avoid coughing.

**Antibiotics:** Ancef, unless contraindicated. This is one of the easier and shorter cases of hernia repairs.  
  
**Possible Complications**  
Infection involving the prosthetic bio-material (mesh).  
Postoperative ileus seromas.  
DVT  
Wound infection.  
Dehiscence with profound coughing during emergence.
