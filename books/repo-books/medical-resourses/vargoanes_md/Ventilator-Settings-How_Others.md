## Ventilator Settings – How others ventilate

**Titrate to ETCO2 and chest excursion  
Note:** Pressure Control Ventilation (PCV) of 20/5 means the Peak Inspiratory Pressure (PIP) is set on 20 cmH20 and the PEEP is set on 5 cmH20.

**Pressure Control Ventilation (PCV)** has become the most popular ventilation mode we seem to use in neonates and infants. Many anesthesia providers appear to convert to Volume Control Ventilation (VCV) for toddlers and up. However, there are still many anesthesia providers that prefer PVC regardless the age.

**Below are just initial ventilator settings and are adjusted immediately according to:**
