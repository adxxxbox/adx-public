# Ancef

**Ancef (Cefazolin, Kefzol)**  
1st Generation Cephalosporin  
The most common antibiotic we administer.  
Extensively used as prophylaxis antibiotic before wide range of surgical operations.  
A Beta-Lactam Antibiotic, so avoid with beta lactam allergy.  
Penicillins and Cephalosporins are Beta-Lactams.  
**Cross Sensitivity b/t Ancef and Penicillins:** up to 10%.  
**Doses**  
**Standard:** 1-2 grams over 3-5 minutes.  
1 gram < 80kg IV  
2 grams > 80kg IV  
**Max:**12 grams/day  
120 kg> 3 grams  
**Redose:** Q4hr with initial pre-op dose.  
**Peds Dosing:**  
25mg/kg IV  
**Infants and Children:** 25-100mg/kg/day IV in divided doses Q6-8h.  
**Max:** 6 grams/day  
  
**MOA**  
Same mode of action as other beta-lactam antibiotics: disrupt the synthesis of the bacterial cell wall.  
  
**Indications**  
For Gram positive cocci: staph and strept-skin infections.  
Ineffective against MRSA or PCN resistant bugs.  
It can also be used to treat moderately severe bacterial infections involving the lung, bone, joint, stomach, blood, heart valve and urinary tract.
