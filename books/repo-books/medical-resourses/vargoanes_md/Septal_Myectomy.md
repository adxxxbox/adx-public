### Septal Myectomy

Septal myectomy is an open-heart procedure to thin out the ventricular septum, which becomes thick and obstructs blood flow in people with Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HOCM).HOCM is the most common genetic heart disorder and cause of sudden cardiac death among the young population and a major cause of disability for patients of any age.  
  
HOCM involves a thickened ventricular septum resulting in a Left Ventricular Outflow Obstruction (LVOT).  
_Refer to” Cardiomyopathy -Hypertrophic with Synopsis of Left Ventricular Outflow Obstruction (LVOTO)” in the “Coexisting Disease and Surgery” App._  
  
**First line of treatment:** Beta-blockers, calcium channel blockers and/or disopyramide.  
**  
Surgical:**  
Alcohol septal ablation or  
Septal myectomy  
  
**Anesthesia: General -** open heart with cardiopulmonary bypass.  
Many of these patients also need their mitral valve repaired (residual leaflet excision) or replaced.  
**Duration:** 3-4 hours  
**Anesthesia set-up:** Same as open heart surgery involving sternotomy and cardiopulmonary bypass.  
**TEE:** Yes  
  
Phenylephrine and volume are your friends!!!

**↓ Preload, ↑ LVOTO:   
The degree of obstruction depends on preload.**   
An under-filled LV causes less separation between the anterior mitral valve leaflet and interventricular septum, resulting in greater obstruction since the LVOT orifice is already narrowed before the onset of systole.   
  
**↓ Afterload, ↑ LVOTO:** \- A decrease in afterload (resistance) to the LV permits an increase in SV and a decrease in ESV(End-systolic volume).  
\- The less ESV, the less separation between the hypertrophied septum and mitral valve. \- It will more likely cause contact with the septum and mitral leaflets, resulting in LVOTO during systole.   
**↑ Contractility, ↑ LVOTO, ↓ CO**   
\- A vigorous contraction will be  **↑SV and ↓ ESV.** \- The less ESV, the less separation between the hypertrophied septum and mitral valve. \- It will more likely cause contact with the septum and mitral leaflets and result in LVOTO with systole.   
\- Cardiac inotropes and stress should be avoided.  
  
**Possible complications:**  
Irregular heart rhythms, such as heart block (may require a pacemaker)  
Infection  
Bleeding  
Cardiac tamponade  
Blood clots leading to stroke or MI  
Complications from anesthesia  
Need for more surgery.  
Removal of too much heart muscle (resulting in VSD)  
Blood flow problems during surgery that can lead to poor pumping function of the heart in the future  
Death
