### AAA-Abdominal Endovascular Aortic Aneurysm Repair (EVAR)

### Room Set-Up

2 large IVs

Arterial line and set-up

**Fluid warmer:** Yes, or have one available

**RBCs available:** Optional (based on anesthesia provider preference)

**Emergency ruptured aneurysm or actively bleeding:** Blood should be in the room.

**Borderline HCT, inexperienced vascular surgeon:** Blood should be available

**Nonurgent procedure:** Type and Screened

**Blood Transfusion Tubing primed:** Same as above

Always have the set-up available

**Pressure bags:** Should be available

**8.0 ETT** is preferred in case the patient remains intubated postoperatively (rare).

**GlideScope:** Optional, as it is less stimulating

**Induction:** Standard, depends on cardiac status

**Analgesia:** This procedure does not require much; it is not stimulating.

Antihypertensive agent infusions:

Nicardipine or NTG + 10cc diluted rescue syringe

Nicardipine continuous infusion is popular to manage postoperative hypertension.

Pressor infusion:

Phenylephrine or norepinephrine + 10 cc diluted rescue syringe

**Note:** Many anesthesia providers prefer norepinephrine.

Heparin will be required as with all vascular cases.

Emergency meds and equipment readily available:

-   Esmolol (Sometimes administered just before direct laryngoscopy)
-   Norepinephrine
-   Epinephrine (Usually not needed)
-   Labetalol (Can be used to treat hypertension with emergence)
-   CaCl should be available
-   Lasix - available
-   Dopamine - available
-   NaHCO3 - available

ACT equipment:

-   Some surgeons or institutions still desire to monitor ACTs.

If it is anesthesia’s responsibility, have the following:

ACT cartridges and the machine

1 cc syringes
