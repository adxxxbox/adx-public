### Power Failure

Power Failure with Functional Back up Generator  
You should do the following once the back up generator is initiated:  
You want to be prepared incase the back up generators also fails  
  
Make sure important equipment is plugged into a red outlet.  
Have light sources available (flashlights, laryngoscope and cell phones).  
Request flashlights if your room does not have any.  
Open the drawers to your Pyxis or electronic drug machine.  
Open drawers to your anesthesia equipment cart.  
Have Ketamine and Propofol available incase you need to convert to TIVA.  
Call the anesthesia tech to bring a portable monitor to your room as back up  
Consider connecting the portable monitor (but not turning it on unless needed).  
Consider a portable suction device on standby.  
If your OR room is the only room affected, call for extension cords to utilized outlets outside the room.  
Alert the appropriate staff to have 2-way radios available.  
  
Power Failure with NO Generator Back Up  
Call Anesthesia help stat  
Light the surgical field (Laryngoscope blade, flashlight or cell phone)  
Call for battery charged portable monitors and suction machine  
  
Electrical Powered Operating Room Phones  
Call for a 2 way radio.  
  
Anesthesia Machine  
Your ventilator should alarm to let you know it is now running on the back-up battery.  
  
To conserve battery life on ventilator:  
Switch from controlled ventilation to spontaneous and manually ventilate the patient via reservoir bag.  
Switch to or use Isoflurane or Sevoflurane.  
TIVA is optional  
  
Convert to paper charting if electronic charting fails  
  
All elective cases are on HOLD ad will not be started until cleared.  
  
Use battery powered portable monitor  
  
Nitrous - no, scavenging system may be down?
