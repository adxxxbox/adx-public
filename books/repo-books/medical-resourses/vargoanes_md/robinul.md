# Robinul

**Robinul (Glycopyrrolate)**  
**Class:** Anticholinergic  
synthetic quaternary amine-does not cross the BBB.  
An Anticholinergic drug that competitively antagonizes the effects of the neurotransmitter Acetylcholine at the muscarinic receptors.  
The anticholinergic drug of choice for antisialogogue effects.  
Antagonizes the GI hypermotility effects of Neostigmine.  
**Onset:** Immediate  
**Doses:**  
**With Reversal:** 0.0 1 -0.02 mg/kg IV or (0.2 mg per 1 mg of Neostigmine).  
  
**Anti-sialogogue:** 0.1 - 0.2 mg IV or 4-6mcg/kg for pre-induction or any patient you may feel at risk for increased tenacious secretions.  
  
**With Ketamine:** 0.2 mg IV is common (antisialogogue)  
  
**Mild Bradycardia (vagolysis) with hypotension:** 0.1-0.2 mg when Ephedrine is not the first choice.  
Given with Neostigmine to prevent Bradycardia  
  
**MOA:**  
Binds to the muscarinic receptor sites and competitively antagonizes (blocks) Acetylcholine at that site. Less acetylcholine = less Bradycardia. Temporary and reversible.  
  
**Indications:**  
Vagolysis (reverse or prevent bradycardia)  
Prevent Bradycardia side effects from Anticholinesterase Neostigmine with muscle relaxant reversal.  
Antisialogogue prior to intubation-especially fiberoptic.  
Bronchospasms - not first choice  
Bradycardia  
  
**Caution with:**  
Glaucoma  
Asthma  
CAD  
GI/GU obstruction  
Parkinsons disease  
  
**Metabolic Oxygen Consumption**  
Robinul: increases  
Atropine: no effect  
Scopolamine: decreases  
Robinul has poor lipid solubility.  
Clearance is more rapid than atropine.  
80% is excreted unchanged in the urine.  
Has the least effect on pupil size of all the anticholinergics.
