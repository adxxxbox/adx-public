### Aortic Valvular Dimensions (area) and Pressure Gradients

**Aortic valvular area dimensions:**  
**Normal Area:**  2.5-4.5 cm2  
**Mild Stenosis:** 1.0-2.5 cm2  
**Moderate Stenosis:**  0.75-1.0 cm2  
**Severe Stenosis:** < 0.75 cm2  
**Symptoms:  
Mild aortic stenosis:** May have mild symptoms**  
Moderate aortic stenosis: Mild symptoms**  
  
**Severe Stenosis:**  
Angina-cardiac  
Dyspnea on exertion-pulmonary  
Orthostatic or exertional syncope (neuro)  
Nonsurgical survival is 2 to 5 years after the onset of symptoms.  
  
**Hemodynamically significant aortic stenosis:** < 1.2 cm2  but not necessarily symptomatic  
**Symptomatic aortic stenosis:** < 0.8-0.9 cm2  
**Critical aortic stenosis:** < 0.7 cm2 (mean gradient >50 mmHg)  
**  
Aortic transvalvular gradient:**  
As stenosis increases, the pressure gradient across the aortic valve increases.  
**  
Aortic valve mean gradient:  
****Normal Gradient:** < 5 mmHg  
**Mild Stenosis:**  5-25 mmHg  
**Moderate Stenosis:**  25-50 mmHg  
  
**Severe Stenosis:**  > 50 mmHg (area < 0.7 cm2)  
With severe stenosis, patients can not appreciably increase their cardiac output.  
Chest compressions are essentially useless.
