# Anticholinesterase Facts

**Edrophonium vs Neostigmine**  
Edrophonium works at the anionic site. This makes it much faster than Neostigmine.  
Edrophonium works in about 3 minutes, and neostigmine works in about 8 minutes.  
Both Edrophonium and Neostigmine are quaternary ammonium structures. With Edrophonium, there is an acetylcholine enzyme that eventually breaks it down. With Neostigmine, there is a carbamylated enzyme that eventually breaks it down. So they're broken down differently.  
Edrophonium is 75% renally cleared.  
You want to give atropine with Edrophonium because their onsets match up better. For the same reason, you want to give Neostigmine with Glycopyrrolate (Robinul).  
**Anticholinesterase Over Dose:**  
Most of them are not that big of a deal, except when you don't add enough anticholinergics for the muscarinic receptors and increase peristalsis leading to a BM as they are waking up.  
In the central nervous system, if you keep giving anticholinesterases, you can get confusion, seizures, coma, and respiratory depression.  
**Treatment for Anticholinesterase Over Dose:**  
Treat with an anticholinergic. The most widely used is Atropine. Give 35-70 ug/kg IV q3-10 minutes until the muscarinic effects disappear. Also, take care of ABCs.
