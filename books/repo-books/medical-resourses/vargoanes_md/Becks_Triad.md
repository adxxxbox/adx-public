### Beck's Triad

Described by the thoracic surgeon Calude S. Beck in 1935.  
  
**It's components are:**  
Distended neck veins  
Distant (muffled) heart sounds  
Hypotension  
  
**Classic sign of cardiac tamponade**  
Rising venous pressure, falling arterial pressure, and decreased heart sounds found in the presence of pericardial tamponade.
