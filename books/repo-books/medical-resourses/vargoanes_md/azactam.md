# Azactam

**Azactam (Aztreonam)**  
**Class:** Monobactam Antibiotic  
A newer synthetic beta-lactam antibiotic  
However, it can still be used safely with patients who are allergic to beta-lactam agents.  
**Note:** With Monobactams- the Beta-lactam ring is alone and not fused to another ring (in contrast to most other Beta-lactams, which have at least two rings). They work only against Gram-negative bacteria.  
**Doses:** 1-2 grams IV over 3-5 minutes  
**Maximum:** 8 grams/day  
**Peds**  
\>1 month: 30-50mg/kg IV Q6-8h  
**Max:**120mg/kg/day or 8 grams/day  
**Advantages of Azactam**  
Not associated with nephrotoxicity  
Excellent against aerobic gram-negative bacteria  
**Disavantage**  
Not useful against gram positive or anaerobic bacteria  
**MOA:** Disrupts cell wall synthesis  
**Side Effects:**  
Diarrhea that is watery or bloody  
Fever, sore throat, and headache with a severe blistering, peeling, and red skin rash.  
Confusion, seizure (convulsions)  
Feeling light-headed, fainting  
Pale skin, easy bruising or bleeding, unusual weakness  
Fever, chills, body aches, flu symptoms and nausea.
