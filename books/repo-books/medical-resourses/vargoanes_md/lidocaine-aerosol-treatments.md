# Lidocaine Aerosol Treatments

**Class:** Local Anesthetic

**Anesthesia Indications:** Anesthetize the upper airway prior to an awake fiberoptic intubation.

**Nebulizer Doses:** 3-5mL of 4% (120-200mg)  
5 mLs of 2% (100mg).  
Nebulize at 6 lpm x 10-20 minutes  

I have seen lidocaine nebulized with Albuterol for patients with reactive airway disease (asthma etc).

There are some reports that inhalation lidocaine can cause an initial bronchoconstriction (histamine-evoked bronchoconstriction).

The combination of lidocaine and Proventil can prevent the initial bronchoconstriction rarely observed with lidocaine alone and can be recommended to prevent bronchoconstriction when airway instrumentation is required.

Attach a facemask on the nebulizer and ask the patient to inhale through their nose if you plan to intubate nasally.

A generally accepted safe range of nebulized lidocaine is between 100 and 200 mg per dose. Carefully monitor of the patient's mental status to avoid lidocaine toxicity.

Lidocaine toxicity is a serious concern, and concentrations of serum lidocaine over 5 mg/L can lead to lightheadedness, tremors, hallucinations, and cardiac arrest.

One study recommending up to 600mg of aerosol lidocaine in asthma patients undergoing experimental bronchoscopy.
