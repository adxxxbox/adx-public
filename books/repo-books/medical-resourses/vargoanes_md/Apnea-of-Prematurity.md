## Apnea of Prematurity

<table border="0" cellpadding="0" width="300"><tbody><tr><td><p>Lasts 20 seconds and results in bradycardia and cyanosis.</p><p>Surgery will make this problem worse due to metabolic and pharmacologic problems.</p><p>Greatest risk is premature infants with PCA (post conceptional age) of less than 44 weeks, children less than 60 wks PCA are hospitalized after surgery.</p><p>Postpone elective surgery if possible.</p><p>Titrate opioids carefully.</p><p>Use regional if possible.</p><p>Combine the immature respiratory neuro control with the respiratory type II muscles (easily fatigued) , this may be responsible for much of the post op apnea - especially with the preterm infants with gestational age &lt;44weeks.</p><p>Prematurely born infants &lt;44 weeks post conception, especially those with a history of apnea, are at high risk (20-40%) of developing post op apnea.</p><p>Caffeine and theophylline reduce apnea, strengthen muscle contractility, strengthen muscle contractility, prevent fatigue, and are respiratory stimulants.</p><p>Refer to Coexisting Diseases &amp; Surgery App for more details and monitoring policies.</p></td></tr></tbody></table>
