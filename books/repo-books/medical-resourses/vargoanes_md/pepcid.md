# Pepcid

**Pepcid (Famotidine)**  
**Class:** Histimine 2 (H2) Receptor Antagonists.  
**Other H2 blockers:** Tagemet (cimetidine) and Zantac (ranitidine).  
**Doses:**  
20mg IV  
20-40mg PO  
**Anesthesia Indications:**  
We mostly use Pepcid in Pre-op for gastric aspiration prophylaxis in high risk patients. Sometimes Reglan is given too.  
**Onset:** < 1 hour  
**Peak:** 0.5-4 hours  
**Duration:** 10-15 hours  
**MOA**  
The acid secreting gastric parietal cells are of the H2 type.  
When these are activated, the proton pump of gastric parietal cells to secrete H ions against a gradient in exchange for K.  
When an H2 receptor antagonist (Pepcid) binds to the H2 receptors, it prevents the gastric parietal cells from secreting H+ ions.  
**Indications:**  
Ulcers or Gerd  
Anaphylaxis  
For anaphylaxis, Pepcid is often given with Benadryl since you want to block BOTH the H1 and H2 receptors.
