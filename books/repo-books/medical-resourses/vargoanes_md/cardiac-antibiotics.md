# Cardiac Antibiotics

**Perioperative Antibiotic Prophylaxis Recommendations For Cardiac, Thoracic, Carotids Large Vascular**  
**Give Ancef if No PCN or Cephalosporin Allergy**  
Infuse 3-5 mins  
Within 60 minutes  
**Dose:** 2 grams x 1  
**Large Vascular:** Give Ancef or Aztreonam (Azactam) if No PCN or Cephalosporin allergy.  
Infuse 3-5 mins  
Within 60 minutes prior to incision  
  
**Dose:** 2 grams x 1  
**Aztreonam (Azactam)**  
3-5 minute vial  
**Minibag:** 20 minutes  
**Dose:** 2 grams IV x 1  
  
**Alternatives to Aztreonam (Azactam) are:**  
Clindamycin 600mg < 80kg and 900mg >80kg  
Gentamycin 5mg/kg (ideal body wt)  
  
**If PCN or Cephalosporin Allergy:**  
**Cardiac, Thoracic, Carotids with PCN/Cephalosporin Allergy:**  
Atreonam (Azactam) or  
Vancomycin  
  
**Vascular:** If PCN/Cephalosporin allergy  
Atreonam (Azactam) or Vancomycin  
**If Allergic to Vancomycin:**  
Clindamycin 600mg < 80kg and 900mg >80kg  
High Risk MRSA  
  
**Vascular:**  
Vancomycin  
  
**What makes MRSA a high risk:**  
Positive nasal swab  
Hx of MRSA  
Currently on antibiotics  
Hospitalized 72hrs  
Transfer from another inpatient facility.
