# Calcium Chloride

**Calcium Chloride (CaCl)**  
CaCl is around 3x more concentrated than Ca Gluconate.  
A 10% CaCl solution has 27mg/ml of elemental calcium.  
10% Calcium Gluconate solution has only 9mg/ml.  
Aways infuse slowly.  
  
**IV Push can cause massive hypertension**  
**Concentration** 1.0-1.3mmol/L  
**Doses:**  
Up 250mg-to 1gram: IV slow push.  
**Peds:**10-20mg/kg  
**Effects:** Increases cardiac contraction and BP.  
  
**Indications:**  
**Hypocalcemia**  
**Hypermagnesemia** (Antidote for overdose of Magnesium Sulfate.  
Magnesium is used for PIH (Preclampsia) to prevent seizures.  
**Hyperkalemia** it reduces the cardiac excitability by being a membrane stabilizer- decreases arrhythmias.  
  
**Contraindications:**  
Ventricular fibrillation  
Hypercalcemia  
  
**Concentration:** 1.0-1.3 mmol/L  
  
**Ca salts are incompatible with:**  
NaHCO3  
Magnesium  
Ceftriaxone  
Dobutamine  
Methylprednisolone  
  
If you dont have a central line, use Calcium Gluconate because Calcium Chloride burns more. Infiltrations are both nasty either way.  
SC, IM routes are NOT USED because of severe sloughing and necrosis.
