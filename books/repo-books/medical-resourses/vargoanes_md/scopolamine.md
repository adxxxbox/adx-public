# Scopolamine

**Scopolamine  
(Levo-duboisine)**  
**Class:** Anticholinergic.  
**Subclass:** tertiary amine (like Atropine)  
From the Brugmansia flower native to Columbia  
Crosses the BBB-CNS effects  
**MOA:**  
Competitive antagonist of ACH at muscarinic receptors, decreases secretions and GI motility.  
**Indications:**  
Prevent awareness for hemodynamically unstable trauma pts.  
The most potent antisialogogue and sedating anticholinergic.  
Produces sedation, amnesia, and euphoria.  
**Trauma doses vary with literature**  
**Doses:** (strength 0.4mg/mL)  
**Trauma Doses:** 5-10mcg/kg is standard.  
Versed may cause HYPOtension.  
**Antiemetic:** 0.3 to 0.65 mg administered IV,IM or SQ q6 -8 hours as needed.  
**Sedation or tranquilization:** 0.6 mg (600 mcg).  
**Pre-op sedation IM/IV:** 0.3 to 0.8 mg (300 to 600 mcg)  
**Antisialogogue:** 0.1-0.2mcg/kg  
**Parkinson's Tremor:** 0.4 to 0.8 mg orally q8h as needed.  
**Peds:**  
Age 6 mo. to 3 yr., 0.1 to 0.15 mg (100 to150 mcg).  
Age 3-6 yr: 0.2 to 0.3mg (200 to 300mcg).  
**Peds N/V (1-12yo):** 6mcg/kg/dose IV,IM or SQ q6 -8h as needed.  
**Onset:** < 30 minutes IV  
**Duration:** 4-6 hours  
**OB/GYN:** Avoid IV scopolamine with pregnancy. Placental transfer is rapid. A standard antiemetic dose of 0.3-0.6mg causes fetal tachycardia and loss of beat to beat variability within 10-25 minutes and lasts 60-90 minutes.  
The Patch is OK -1 hour before a scheduled C-sec is recommended.  
**Central Cholinergic Syndrome-**Scopolamine may cause it.  
Be aware that Scopolamine will alter the pupillary exam because of its anticholinergic effects.  
**Treatment:** Physostigmine 0.1-0.3mcg/kg and may repeat 15-30 minutes.  
**Antisialogogue:** 3x more potent than atropine. But Robinul is your first choice.  
**Sedation (decreasing RES):** 100x more potent than atropine  
**Amnestic:** Yes  
**Side Effects:**  
Central Cholinergic Syndrome  
pain/redness to the eyes with dilated pupils  
difficulty urinating  
drowsiness  
dizziness  
dry mouth  
restlessness  
blurred vision  
dry or itchy eyes  
flushing  
tachycardia  
Potent mydriatic effects-avoid with glaucoma.
