### Oral Activated Charcoal

Oral activated charcoal is rarely used with overdoses of anticoagulants but still mentioned in some literature.  
  
Oral activated charcoal, 100 Gm PO/NG ×1, is an option to reduce absorption for all Direct Oral Anticoagulants (DOACs) in the appropriate patient.  
  
**Charcoal can be considered:**  
When Rivaroxaban was taken within 8 hours.  
When Apixaban was taken within 6 hours.  
When Edoxaban and Dabigatran was taken within 2 hours.  
  
**_OR_  
  
Charcoal can be considered when:**  
Rivaroxaban was taken within 8 hours.  
Apixaban was taken within 6 hours.  
Edoxaban and Dabigatran was taken within 2 hours.
