# Bumex vs Lasix

**Difference between Bumex and Lasix**  
Bumex is 40 -50x more potent than Lasix on a weight basis.  
Bumex is less potent with urinary potassium excretion  
Bumex is absorbed more quickly than lasix and is twice as bioavailable.  
In both CHF and normal subjects, more bumex than lasix was absorbed.  
The elimination half-life of lasix was twice that of Bumex  
However, in CHF patients, overall response from both drugs did not really differ. Oral or IV Bumex of 0.5-2mg/day is comparable to 20-80mg/day of lasix.  
Patients with edema from renal disease respond better to Bumex.  
Potassium supplements and spironolactone may be beneficial additions to bumex where patients at risk of hypokalaemia.
