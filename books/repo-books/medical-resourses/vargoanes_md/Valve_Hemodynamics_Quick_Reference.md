### Valve Hemodynamics Quick Reference

**Aortic Stenosis - The worst!**  
**Preload:** Keep elevated.  
**Afterload:** Maintain to high SVR  
**HR:** Maintain 50-80bpm.  
**Rhythm:** NSR  
_Patients with AS do NOT tolerate hypotension.  
Neuraxial anesthesia is relatively contraindicated._

**Aortic Insufficiency:**  
**Preload:** Keep elevated.  
**Afterload:** Down-lower SVR  
**HR:** 60-80  
**Rhythm:** NSR  
_Aortic Regurgitation/Insufficiency may NOT tolerate bradycardia and in increased SVR.  
  
_**"Fast Full Forward"**  
_Aortic Regurgitation/Insufficiency may NOT tolerate bradycardia and in increased SVR._  
Full, mildly vasodilated and modest tachycardia is an optimal cardiovascular state.

**Mitral Stenosis:**  
**Preload:** Keep elevated.  
**Afterload:** Maintain SVR  
**HR:** maintain 50-80bpm  
**Rhythm:** NSR  
  
_Prevent and treat events that could decrease cardiac output (CO) and produce pulmonary edema.  
A sudden decrease in systemic vascular resistance (SVR) may not be tolerated._

**Mitral Regurgitation**  
**Preload:** Keep elevated.  
**Afterload:** Lower SVR  
**HR:** Maintain 50-80bpm  
**Rhythm:** NSR  
**Goal:** Improve left ventricular stroke volume
