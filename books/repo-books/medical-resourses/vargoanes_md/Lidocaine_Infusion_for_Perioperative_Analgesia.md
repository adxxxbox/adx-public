### Lidocaine Infusion for Perioperative Analgesia

The exact mechanism of IV lidocaine that provides systemic analgesia remains largely unknown.  
  
**Perioperative continuous infusions doses of IV lidocaine  
Intravenous bolus** : 1-2 mg/kg followed by-  
**Continuous infusion:**  0.5-3 mg/kg/hour  
_The most widely reported and clinically effective dose range appears to be from 1- 2 mg/kg/hour._  
  
**Acute pain management** : IV lidocaine infusion demonstrates analgesia, anti-hyperalgesia, and anti-inflammatory properties.  
  
**Quick notes of IV lidocaine  
**May be an effective analgesic adjuvant for perioperative pain.  
May reduce the incidence of postoperative nausea and vomiting, pruritus, and urinary retention.  
May decrease the intensity of postoperative pain.  
Reduces the postoperative opioid consumption.Is a potent anti-inflammatory, anti-hyperalgesia, and gastrointestinal pro-peristaltic drug.  
GI surgical procedures: It decreased pain scores, opioid analgesic consumption, and side-effects.Acute pain adjunct to achieve enhanced recovery after surgery outcomes.  
Can be safely infused over several days postoperatively for carefully selected and monitored patients.  
  
**Contraindications of Lidocaine:**  
Unstable coronary disease   
Recent MI  
Heart failure  
Cardiac ejection fraction < 20%  
Heart block  
Electrolyte disturbances  
Liver disease  
Cardiac arrhythmia disorders   
Seizure disorders  
Severe hepatic impairment (bilirubin > 1.46 mg/dL)  
Severe renal impairment  
Acute porphyria
