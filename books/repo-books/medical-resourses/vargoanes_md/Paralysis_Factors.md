### Factors That May Prolong Paralysis

  
_(Supplement to AANA Journal, 2016; p. 8)_

**Pathophysiologic Causes  
**Acid Maltase Deficiency (Pompe Disease) (Glycogen-storage disease type II)  
Acidosis  
Adrenocortical Dysfunction  
Acute Intermittent Porphyria (AIP) - rare metabolic disorder  
Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (Lou Gehrig's Disease)  
Anoxia and Ischemia  
Carcinomatous Polyneuropathy - _A condition which damages nerves by directly invading or putting pressure on them or by triggering an autoimmune reaction._  
Cholinesterase Deficiency (Pseudocholinesterase)  
Compressive (Entrapment) Neuropathy - _caused by direct pressure on a single nerve._  
Critical illness polyneuropathy - _disease of peripheral nerves, occurring as a complication of severe trauma or infection (critical illness). Common in ICU patients and diagnosed by limb weakness and unexplained difficulty in weaning from mechanical ventilation._  
Diphtheria  
Eaton-Lambert Syndrome - Autoimmune disorder- _nerves cells become unable to release enough of acetylcholine_.  
Guillain-Barr'e Syndrome  
Hypercarbia

**Electrolyte/Lab Abnormalities**  
Hypokalemia  
Hypocalcemia  
Hypomagnesemia  
Hypophosphatemia  
Hypothermia  
Thiamine Deficiency  
Vitamin E Deficiency  
Motor Neuron Disease  
Multiple Sclerosis  
Muscular Dystrophy  
Myasthenia Gravis  
Myotonic Syndromes  
Neurofibromatosis  
Nonspecific Nutritional Deficiency  
Poliomyelitis  
Pyridoxine abuse (vitamin B6) - _megavitaminosis_  
Polymyositis  
Renal Failure (use Nimbex or Tracrium)  
Sepsis  
Trauma  
Wound botulism

**Pharmacological  
**Aminoglycoside toxicity -Gentamycin  
Penicillin Toxicity  
Steroid Myopathy (corticosteroid induced)

**Antihypertensives  
**Ca-Channel blockers  
Beta blockers  
Lasix (Furosemide)

**Antidysrhythmics  
**Quinidine  
Procainamide  
Local anesthetics (large doses)

**Antibiotics  
**Aminoglycosides  
Tetracycline  
Polymyxin b  
Clindamycin

**Others  
**Cyclosporine  
Dantrolene and Ryanodex  
Azathioprine - immunosuppressive drug used in organ transplantation  
Steroids  
Magnesium  
Organophosphate (poisoning) - _some insecticides, nerve gases, some ophthalmic agents and some herbicides._  
Volatile anesthetics  
Lithium
