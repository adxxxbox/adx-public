# Cefoxitin

**Cefoxitin (Mefoxin)**  
2nd Generation Cephalosporin  
A cephamycin  
A Beta-Lactam Antibiotic, so avoid with beta lactam allergy.  
Penicillins and Cephalosporins are Beta-Lactams.  
**Doses**  
1 gram < 80kg IV give over 30 mins  
2 grams > 80kg IV give over 30 mins  
**Max:** 12 grams per day  
**Redose:** Q4h with initial dose  
**Peds Doses**  
\> 3 months: 80 to 160 mg/kg of body weight per day divided into four to six equal doses.  
**Max:**12 grams/day.  
**Aerobic Gram positive:** weaker than 1st generation  
**Aerobic Gram negative:** Stronger than 1st generation  
**Surgical Prophylaxis Indications:**  
Uncontaminated gastrointestinal surgery  
Vaginal hysterectomy,  
Abdominal hysterectomy  
Cesarean section.  
**Beside prophylaxis, it is for treating:**  
Lower respiratory tract infections, including pneumonia and lung abscess.  
Urinary tract infections  
Intra-abdominal infections  
Gynecological infections  
Septicemia  
Bone and joint infections  
Skin and skin structure infections  
**Side Effects**  
Gastrointestinal upset and diarrhea  
**Allergic Reactions**  
Anaphylaxis, interstitial nephritis and angioedema have been noted. Hypotension  
Possible exacerbation of myasthenia gravis.  
Transient elevations in SGOT, SGPT, serum LDH, and serum alkaline phosphatase; and jaundice have been reported.  
Elevations in serum Creatine /or BUN levels have been observed. As with the cephalosporins, acute renal failure has been reported rarely.
