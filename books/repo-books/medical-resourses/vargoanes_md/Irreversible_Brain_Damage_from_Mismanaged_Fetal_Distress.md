### Irreversible Brain Damage from Mismanaged Fetal Distress

Any complication during labor may cause oxygen deprivation in the fetus may result in cerebral palsy, hypoxic ischemic encephalopathy (HIE), periventricular leukomalacia (PVL) and intellectual and developmental disabilities.  
  
Asphyxia by definition involves both hypoxia and hypercapnia with metabolic acidosis.  
Many studies suggest that the periods of hypotension during severe asphyxia is associated with decreased brain perfusion that leads to the brain damage.  
  
Unfortunately, it is difficult to tell exactly how long the fetus can tolerate asphyxia without brain damage due to the variations of asphyxia and compensatory mechanisms involved.  
  
When facing the onset of severe fetal distress, nobody really knows how much time you really have to get the baby out before there is irreversible brain damage. We normally just get to surgery and get the baby out ASAP.  
  
Always have emergency intubation equipment (even a Glide scope) and meds available.
