### VACTERL Association

A disorder that affects many body systems.  
VACTERL association occurs in 1 in 10,000 to 40,000 newborns.  

**VACTERL stands for :**  
V- Vertebral defects  
A- Anal atresia  
C- Cardiac defects,  
T- Tracheo-esophageal fistula  
R- Renal anomalies  
L- Limb abnormalities.

People diagnosed with VACTERL association typically have at least three of these characteristic features. Affected individuals may have additional abnormalities that are not among the characteristic features of VACTERL association.  
  
**Vertebral defects:** present in 60% – 80% of people with VACTERL association. These include:

Misshapen vertebrae  
Fused vertebrae  
Missing or extra vertebrae

**Anal atresia**  
60-90% of people with VACTERL association have narrowing or blockage of the anus (anal atresia).  
Anal atresia may also be accompanied with genitalia and urinary tract anomalies.  
  
**Cardiac**  
Heart defects occur in 40 – 80% of people with VACTERL association. Can be subtle to life threatening.  

**Tracheo-Esophageal (T-E) Fistula  
**50-80% of people with VACTERL association have a TE Fistula.

**Renal**  
Kidney anomalies occur in 50 – 80% of people with VACTERL association.

Affected individuals may be missing one or both kidneys  
An abnormally developed or misshapen kidneys-may affect kidney function.

**Limb**  
Limb abnormalities are seen in 40 – 50% of people with VACTERL association.

Poorly developed or missing thumbs  
Underdeveloped forearms and hands.

Some of the features of VACTERL association can be subtle and are not identified until late in childhood or adulthood, making diagnosis of this condition difficult.
