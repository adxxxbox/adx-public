# Decadron

**Decadron (Dexamethasone)**  
**Class:** Glucocorticoid Steroid  
A Flourinated derivative of Prednisilone.  
**Doses:**  
**Prophylaxis PONV:** 4mg  
**Peds:** 0.15mg/kg  
**EENT Peds:** 12 mg-common dose.  
**Cranies/Backs:** cerebral/spinal edema-10mg.  
**Shock:** Up to 40mg.  
**Airway Edema:** 4-12mg (EENT or difficult intubations).  
**Prevent allergic reactions:** 4-8mg.  
4mg Equivalent Dose equals 4mg Zofran or 1.25mg Droperidol.  
**When to give:**  
Most effective before induction  
**Onset:** 10-30 minutes  
**1/2life:** 200 minutes  
Tissue Level persists for 72 hours  
**Caution with:**  
Diabetics increases blood sugar.  
Infected patients: Delays wound healing.  
**Elimination halftime:** 3 hours  
**Adverse Reactions:**  
**General Indications:**  
Anti-inflammatory  
Immunosuppressant  
Antiemetic  
**Anesthesia Related Indications:**  
Prophylaxis for PONV  
Airway Swelling-difficult intubations and EENT cases.  
Decrease cerebral and spinal swelling-Crani and backs.  
Help prevent allergic reactions  
Shock-large doses  
**Other Notes**  
26 times more potent than natural human cortisol.  
6.6 times more potent than prednisone.  
Suppresses chemo-therapy induced nausea and vomiting.  
Exerts anti-inflammatory and immunosuppressant effects.  
Prevents PONV when given early on the case probably by reducing surgery-induced inflammation due to the inhibition of prostaglandin synthesis.  
Also may exert antiemetic effects by increasing endorphins resulting in mood elevations and appetite stimulation.  
**Other indications:**  
Allergic anaphylactic shock-high doses  
ITP  
Eye drops  
Nasal Spray
