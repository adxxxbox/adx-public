# Cipro

**Cipro (Ciprofloxacin)**  
2nd Generation Quinolone (just like Levaquin).  
Synthetic broad spectrum antimicrobial.  
The most potent quinolone against aerobic gram negative.  
**Doses:**  
400mg IV over 60 minutes  
200-400mg IV q8-12h  
**Peds:** not approved for <16 years  
**MOA**  
Inhibiting DNA synthesis  
**Avoid giving Cipro when:**  
Patient is taking Tizanidine (Zanaflex)  
History of Myasthenia ravis  
Theophylline-can elevate Theophylline levels  
**Allergic to Flouroquinolones:**  
Gemifloxacin (Factive)  
Levofloxacin (Levaquin)  
Moxifloxacin (Avelox)  
Norfloxacin (Noroxin)  
Coumadin and Cipro (quinolones) Interaction- May increases INR and PT.  
**Coumadin and quinolone interaction**  
The exact mechanism of the interaction is unknown. Reduction of intestinal flora responsible for vitamin K production by antibiotics is probable as well as decreased metabolism and clearance of coumadin.  
Cipro has also been linked to tendinitis and even rupture of tendons, particularly the Achilles tendon.  
Sun sensitivity- patients will become more sensitive to the sun. They should avoid prolonged sun exposure, tanning booths, and sunlamps.  
Can be given for: Prostate biopsy as primary antibiotic. Commonly given with Clindamycin when there is a beta-lactam allergy.  
**Indicated for:**  
Urinary Tract Infections  
Acute Uncomplicated Cystitis in females  
Chronic Bacterial Prostatitis  
Acute Sinusitis  
Skin and Skin Structure Infections  
Bone and Joint Infections  
Complicated Intra-Abdominal Infections  
Infectious Diarrhea  
Uncomplicated cervical and urethral gonorrhea  
Pediatric patients (1 to 17 years of age)  
Complicated Urinary Tract Infections and Pyelonephritis  
**PREGNANCY:** Ciprofloxacin should be avoided in pregnancy, as safe use in pregnant women has not been established.  
This medication passes into breast milk.
