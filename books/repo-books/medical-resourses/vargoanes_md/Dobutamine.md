# Dobutamine

**Dobutamine (Dobutrex)**  
Not as popular than it once was because it can cause tachycardia. Milrinone appears to be slowly replacing Dobutamine.  
**Class:** Inotrope/Pressor  
**Doses: (always a drip) titrate to effect**  
2.5-20mcg/kg/min  
**Max:** 40mcg/kg/min  
**Peds:** 2-20mcg/kg/min  
Titrate in increments of 2.5mcg/kg/min Q 10mins.  
**Concentrations:**  
500mg/250mL bag = 2,000mcg/mL  
1,000mg/250mL bag = 4,000mcg/mL  
**Increases:** BP, HR, CO/CI, SV and CVP  
**Decreases:** SVR  
Potent nonselective Beta agonist (mostly B1)  
Some alpha agonist  
Synthetic Catecholamine (Sympathomimetic)  
**MOA:**  
Beta 1 agonist, increases contractility  
Some increases HR  
Peripheral vasoconstriction  
**Indications:**  
To increase CO/CI (esp. when coming off pump).  
Cardiogenic shock  
Refractory CHF  
**Half-life:** 2 minutes  
**Excretion:** Urine as metabolites  
**Precautions:** Ventricular irritability  
Post MI  
Can be deactivated by alkaline solutions.
