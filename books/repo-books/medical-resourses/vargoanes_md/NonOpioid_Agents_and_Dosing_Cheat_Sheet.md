### Non-Opioid Agents and Dosing Cheat Sheet

| Agent | MOA | Dosing | Comments |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Tylenol  
(acetaminophen)  
**IV:** Ofirmev** | Centrally acting analgesic  
Possible COX inhibitor | **≥ 50 kg:** 1,000 mg PO or IV  
**< 50 kg:**  
650 mg PO or  
10 mg/kg IV  
  
**Daily max:** 4 grams in 24 hours for patients  
≥ 50 kg | Caution in liver failure patients  
Acetaminophen is a non-aspirin pain reliever |
| **Ibuprofen  
(Motrin, Advil)** | COX inhibitor | **Pre-op:** 600-800 mg PO  
**Post-op:** 600 mg 6h | **Caution with:**  
GI ulcers  
Kidney disease  
Coagulopathy  
Celebrex can be substituted for Ibuprofen to reduce risk for bleeding. |
| **Toradol  
(ketrorolac)** | COX inhibitor | 10-15 mg PO or IV  
Intra or pre-op | 30 mg is no longer the standard.  
Caution with GI ulcers  
Avoid when Cr Cl > 1.0  
Ask the surgeon before administering towards the end of the surgical procedure |
| **Celebrex  
(celecoxib)** | Selective COX-2  
inhibitor | **Pre-op:** 400 mg PO  
**Post-op:** 200 mg Q 12h | ↑ risk for MI  
Avoid with CHF  
↓risk for GI bleed vs the following non-selective COX inhibitors:  
ibuprofen  
naproxin  
diclofenac  
_Avoid with in patients with sulfa allergies._ |
| **Meloxicam  
(Mobic, Vivlodex, Mobicox)** | A newer COX-2 inhibitor | **Dose:** 15 mg PO  
(max dose 15 mg/day) | **Caution with:**  
Coagulopathy  
Cardiac disease  
Renal disease |
| **Neurontin  
(gabapentin)** | Inhibits voltage-gated Ca channels | **Pre-op:** PO  
600-1,200 mg PO  
1-2 hours preoperatively  
  
**Post-op:** 300 mg PO Q8h | A **Gabapentinoid:** An anti-epileptic medications used to treat seizures and nerve pain (neuropathic pain).  
↓ dose with ↓ CrCl  
May cause sedation in elderly. |
| **Lyrica  
(pregabalin)** | Inhibits voltage-gated Ca channels | **Dose:** 150-300 mg/day oral (2.5 mg/kg) 1-2 hours preoperatively | A **Gabapentinoid:** An anti-epileptic medication used to treat seizures and nerve pain (neuropathic pain).  
↓ Dose with ↓ CrCl  
May cause sedation in elderly.  
Lyrica is absorbed faster with a faster onset than gabapentin._  
(The DEA has classified Lyrica as a controlled substance (listed in Schedule V)._ |
| **Ketamine  
(Ketalar)** | NMDA antagonist | **Intra-op:**  
5-10 mg boluses up to 0.5 mg/kg  
**Continuous infusion:**  
_Refer to "Ketamine Dose Cheat Sheet"_ | Small intra-op boluses are common as needed.  
Most effective in surgeries resulting moderate to severe pain |
| **Methocarbamol  
(Robaxin)** | Unknown | 1 gram IV or PO | **Skeletal Muscle Relaxant**  
Helps ↓ back muscle spasms |
| **Lidocaine  
(IV infusion)** | Na channel blocker | 1-2 mg/kg bolus followed by 0.5-3 mg/kg/hour.  
1-2 mg/kg/hr is popular | **Contraindications:**  
Unstable coronary disease  
Recent MI  
Heart failure  
Ejection fraction < 20%  
Heart block  
Electrolyte disturbances  
Liver disease  
Cardiac arrhythmia disorders |
| **Precedex  
Dexmedetomidine** | Pre and post synaptic  
Alpha-2 receptor agonist in the locus coeruleus | 0.1-0.5 mcg/kg/hour after induction | It may cause bradycardia and hypotension.  
Avoid with bradycardia |
| **Magnesium  
Sulfate (MgSO4)** | Blocks NMDA receptors in a voltage-dependent manner | 30-50 mg/kg over 15 minutes followed by  
6-20 mg/kg/hour.  
  
_Refer to "Magnesium  
Sulfate Analgesia" for details._ | **Normal Mg levels:**  
0.7-1.3 mmol/L or  
1.7-2.7 mg/dL  
Hypermagnesemia occurs rarely in clinical medicine unless the renal function of the patient is compromised. |
