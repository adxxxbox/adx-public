# Benadryl

**Benadryl (Diphenhydramine)**  
**Class:** First Generation Histimine 1(H1) Receptor antagonist.  
Central antimuscarinic action.  
**Doses:** IV  
**Adult:** 10-50mg  
**Common Dose:** Start with 6.25mg - 12.5mg.  
May slow emergence.  
**Peds:** 5mg/kg/day  
**Peds - Pruritus:** 0.5-1mg/kg  
**Peds - Antiemetic or sedation:** 1mg/kg  
25-50mg PO/IM  
  
**Indications:**  
Primarily for allergic reactions.  
**Anaphylaxis:** Give both, H1 and H2 Antagonists stat.  
Can be given prophylactically-IV Dye cases.  
Sneezing and nasal ocular itching.  
Also for (nausea) antiemetic and sedation.  
  
**Warning:** can slow emergence when given during a case.  
**Onset:** < 30 minutes IV  
**Duration:** 4-8 hours  
**Sedative Effects:** Yes  
**Anticholinergic Activity:** Yes  
**Antiemetic Effects:** Yes  
**Protein Binding:** 78-99%  
  
Patient should be off MAOI Inhibitors; such as isocarboxazid (Marplan), phenelzine (Nardil), or tranylcypromine (Parnate) in the last 14 days.  
  
**Adverse Reactions:**  
Urinary retention  
Over sedation  
Hypotension  
Wheezing  
Bradycardia  
  
**Caution with:**  
Asthma  
Seizure disorders  
Glaucoma and Increased IOP  
Stomach ulcer  
BPH  
  
**When H1 Receptors are activated:**  
Increases intracellular cGMP  
Bronchoconstriction  
Coronary artery vasoconstriction  
Slows cardiac impulses through the AV node.  
Mediates release of prostacyclin.  
Increases capillary (with H2).  
Peripheral vascular Dilation (with H2).  
  
**First generation H1 receptor antagonists can cause:**  
Sedation  
Activate muscarinic receptors  
Activate alpha-adrenergic receptors.
