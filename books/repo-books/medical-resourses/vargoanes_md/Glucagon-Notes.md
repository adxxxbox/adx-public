# Glucagon

**Glucagon**  
**Class:** Hormone (antihypoglycemic agent).  
A natural pancreatic hormone that helps regulate blood sugar.  
Causes transient relaxation of the smooth muscles of the GI tract  
and the ampulla of Vater.  
**Anesthesia Indications:**  
We almost always just use it during ERCPs when the GI doc can not penetrate a closed (contracted) ampulla of Vater.  
Intraoperative cholangiography (IOC) with cholecystectomy.  
**Onset:** 1 minute  
**Duration:** 20-30 mintutes.  
**Doses:**  
Usually up to 1mg (unit) IV  
Dilute 1 mL to the small powdered vial= 1mg(unit) per mL.  
**Other Notes:**  
**Actions:**  
Causes breakdown of glycogen to glucose.  
Inhibits glycogen synthesis.  
Elevates blood glucose level.  
Increases cardiac contractile force.  
Increases heart rate  
**Indications**  
**Antidote-Beta-blocker overdose**  
Separate glucagon receptors stimulate adenyl cyclase improving. heart rate, blood pressure and conduction defects.  
**Antidote for Calcium Channel blocker overdose:**  
Initial dose of glucagon is 3 mg IV over 1 minute.
