### Toe or Foot Amputation

**Most of these patients have comorbidities such as:**  
PVD (possible CAD)  
HTN  
ESRD  
Extremity ischemia  
Many have poor compliance and still smoke. Many have scaly, dry skin, non-healing and/or infected sores on their legs or feet due to diabetes, HTN, PVD, or all 3.

**Anesthesia:** General/LMA or ETT  
**Regional:** Optional; an Ankle block for a toe amputation is sometimes done.  
**Regional for ankle amputation:** spinal or popliteal, sciatic and saphenous.  
**Duration:** 30-60 minutes; the occasional Podiatrist may take longer.  
Position: Supine with arms out.  
**IV Access:** 20G  
**EBL:** Minimal if they use a tourniquet.  
  
**Possible Complications:**  
Slow healing  
DVT  
Wound Infection
