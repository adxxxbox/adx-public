# Sevoflurane

**Sevoflurane (Ultane)**  
Flourinated methyl ispropyl ether  
Standard inhalation induction agent for peds.  
Nonpungent, gas of choice for RAD (reactive airway disease).  
**Color Code:** Yellew  
**Blood:** Gas Partition Coefficient is 0.69. This means low blood solubility and fast onset/offset.  
**MAC (30-55yo):** 1.8%  
**MAC (>60yo):** 1.7%  
**Peds MAC:** 2.3-3.5%  
**Vapor Pressure:** 170  
**Prevents Recall:** 0.5 MAC  
**Duration:** Recovery from Sevoflurane is 3-4 minutes faster than Isoflurane. This is magnified in cases >3 hours.  
**Forms Compound A:** yes, when broken down  
**Forms CO:** the LEAST likely as compared to the other agents  
**More Notes:**  
Produces brochodilitation  
Causes the least degree of airway irritation.  
When exposed to strong bases present in CO2 absorbents, it breaks down to Compound A (causing renal tubular injury in rats). Be careful with kidney patients.  
So there is a theoretical potential for renal toxicity from inorganic fluoride metabolites.  
Literature states to run flows at >2LPM  
Sevo and Halothane are acceptable for inhalation induction.  
A lot cheaper now if the generic brand is used.
