# Sodium Bicarbonate

**Sodium Bicarbonate (NaHCO3)**  
**MOA:** When given IV, it increases plasma bicarbonate, buffers excess hydrogen ion concentration, raises blood pH and reverses the clinical manifestations of acidosis.  
**Anesthesia Related Indications:**  
Treat Metabolic Acidosis  
To prevent contrast-Induced Kidney Injury  
Cardiac Arrest  
CABGs  
  
**Doses:**  
**Cardiac Arrest:** 1 mEq/kg/dose IV push boluses.  
**Maintenance:** 0.5 mEq/kg/dose q10min or as indicated by arterial blood gases.  
  
**To prevent contrast-Induced Kidney Injury:  
(Usually when Cr >1 before a procedure involving IV Dye)**  
  
1 hour bolus before procedure: Start at 3.5 mL/kg of a 130 mEq/Liter concentration for 1 hour. (but do not exceed 110 Kg when calculating the bolus dose).  
If significant BP increase occurs, stop the bolus and diurese the patient.  
After the blood pressure stabilizes, an alternative hydration protocol should be chosen (e.g., half normal saline) prior to radiocontrast imaging procedure, at the physician's discretion.  
After 1 hour, decrease drip rate to 1mL/kg/hr x 6 hrs.  
**Treat Metabolic Acidosis (Usually when pH < 7.1 and HC03 < 14mEq/L)**  
wt(kg) x BE x 0.3= dose in mEq  
**Incompatibilties**  
Norepinephrine and dobutamine cannot be used as additives in an IV with Bicarb.  
Don't mix Bicarb with Ca because it may precipitate.  
**Possible Adverse Effects**  
Tetany  
Hypernatremia  
Alkalosis  
Gastric distension  
Hypocalcemia  
Hyperosmolality  
Milk-alkali syndrome  
Volume Overload  
Pulmonary edema  
  
**Contraindications**  
Alkalosis (pH greater than 7.45).  
Hypernatremia (serum sodium greater than 145 mmol/L).  
Hypocalcemia (corrected calcium less than 8 mg/dL).  
Unknown abdominal pain.  
  
**More Notes**  
**Non Anesthesia Related Indications**  
Tricyclic Antidepressant Toxicity.  
Barbiturate Toxicity  
Cocaine Toxicity  
Salicylate Toxicity  
Mucolytic in respiratory treatments.  
**Doses for Non Anesthesia Related**  
**Tricyclic Antidepressant Toxicity**  
Use if QRS >100-200 ms, or hypotension  
Initial bolus: 1-2 mEq/kg IV push over 1-2 minutes; not to exceed 100 mEq/dose.  
Follow-up infusion: 100-150 mEq in 1 L D5/0.45% NaCl infused 100-200 mL/hr IV; titrate infusion to achieve blood pH of 7.45-7.55  
**Cocaine Toxicity**  
1-2 mEq/kg IV push boluses  
**Salicylate Toxicity**  
100-150 mEq IV with 40-80 mEq of potassium chloride in each liter of D5W; administer at 150-250 mL/hr to maintain higher than normal urine output.  
**Barbiturate Toxicity**  
1 mEq/kg IV bolus, followed by an IV drip of 1000 mL of D5W to which 2-3 ampules of sodium bicarbonate are added, initiate drip rate at 200 mL/hr, titrate drip rate to blood pH (7.45) and urinary pH (7.5-8).  
Ventilation must be adequate to eliminate the CO2 produced from bicarbonate.  
Bicarb may cause clinical deterioration if tissue hypoxia is present  
Bicarb is probably not useful in most cases of high anion gap acidosis.
