Oxygen content may be calculated for both venous blood and arterial blood.  In fact, the difference between the two will become very important in figuring out oxygen consumption and extraction. 

O2 content can be thought of as the sum of two means of oxygen transport in the blood:  
98.5% of the oxygen that is carried on the hemoglobin (SaO2)  
1.5% of the oxygen that is carried in the blood (PaO2)  
  
The calculation, using the values listed above in the chart is as follows:

**O2 content** = (gm Hbg)(1.34 ml O2/gm Hbg)(% sat) + 0.003(pO2) = ml O2/dL

Arterial oxygen content  (equation) = (Hgb x 1.36 x SaO2) + (0.0031 x PaO2)

**SaO2** = % of hemoglobin saturated with oxygen  
(Normal range: 93-100%)

**Hgb** = Hemoglobin  
Normal range(Adults): Male: 13-18 g/dl   
                                   Female: 12-16 g/dl

**PaO2**\= Arterial oxygen partial pressure  
(Normal range: 80-100)

**CaO2**: Directly reflects the total number of oxygen molecules in arterial blood (both bound and unbound to hemoglobin)

**NOTE:** The pO2 contributes very little to the oxygen content of blood, having little impact on arterial blood oxygenation.  The PaO2 should be used more for assessing pulmonary (or oxygenator) gas exchange.
