# Amicar

**Amicar (Aminocaproic Acid)**  
Usually only given during heart cases.  
**Class:** Hemostatic Agent, antifibrinolytic.  
**Indications:**  
**In Anesthesia:** used to prevent bleeding after heart surgery.  
There is many different ways to infuse it. Varies from place to place.  
**MOA:**  
Inhibits activation of plasminogen -reducing conversion of plasminogen to plasmin (fibrinolysin). So it prevents the breakdown of fibrin clots.  
It also directly inhibits plasmin activity, but higher doses are required than are needed to reduce plasmin formation.  
**Doses:** vary  
**2 Common Methods of giving Amicar during a heart procedure (there are more ways)**  
You bolus twice and perfusionist gives a bolus while on pump  
15 grams Total:  
5 grams post induction bolus by anesthesia.  
5 grams by perfusionist bolus by perfusionist.  
5 grams post protamine bolus by anesthesia.  
Some just slow IV push 5 grams and start a drip at 50cc/hr (simple method)  
5 bottles amicar to 150 ml bag = 100 mg/ml  
Loading dose is 0.5 ml/kg over 30 min  
followed by 0.25 ml/kg/hr until post op  
**Max Dose:** 30 grams/day  
**Adverse Effects:** HYPOtension, bradycardia, arrhythmias.  
Do not infuse amicar into the same line as blood.  
Amicar is given after induction during open heart surgery and finished at the end of the case or in cardiac recovery.  
It's competitor "Aprotinin" is no longer on the market in the US.
