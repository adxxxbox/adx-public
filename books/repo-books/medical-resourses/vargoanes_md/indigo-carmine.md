# Indigo Carmine

**Indigo Carmine (Indigotindisulfonate Sodium)**  
A dye given IV, appearing in the urine within 10 minutes.  
During a cystoscopy, when the dye can be observed draining from both ureters into the bladder, it confirms patent and functional ureters.  
**Dose:**  
The entire vial  
5mL of 0.8% solution (40mg) IV push  
**IM:** larger quantities needed  
**Peds:** use smaller dose and for under weight patients  
**Excreted:** kidneys  
**Half life:** 4-5 minutes  
**Adverse Effects:** HTN (A mild pressor)  
**Indications:**  
To evaluate ureteral orifices or to check for ureteral injury intraoperatively during cystoscopy.  
Leak Test for Gastric Bypass procedures (we pour it diluted down the lavage tube).  
Originally utilized as a kidney function test  
**CONTRAINDICATIONS**  
Indigo Carmine is contraindicated in patients who have previously experienced an adverse reaction following its use.  
**WARNINGS**  
An occasional idiosyncratic drug reaction may occur.
