### Cranitomy Room Set-up Quick Check list

Refer to _“Craniotomy Room and Medication Doses Set-up-Adults”_ for more details.

Note: Below is just a guide as room set-ups vary between surgeon preference, institutional policies and the patient situation.

**Equipment**

-   ETT #7 and 8 with stylet
-   Esophageal or skin temp probe
-   Glide Scope should be available.
-   Tegaderm for covering the eyes
-   Tape for ETT
-   Accordion extension for ETT (50 cc flex tube)
-   1-2 pressure cables for arterial line and ICP

-   Two **Propofol syringes** (one for stimulation from the Mayfield pin placement)
-   A 10 cc pressor **syringe (Norepinephrine or Neosynephrine)** from the hanging bag
-   A 10 cc **Nicardipine syringe** from the hanging bag
-   **Fentanyl** syringe (usually 100 mcg)
-   Muscle Relaxants **(Norcuron or Rocuronium)**
-   2 grams of **Ancef** (if not contraindicated)
-   1 gram of **Vancomycin** (250 cc bag)
-   **Keppra (Levetiracetam)** 1 gram over 2 minutes – Check MAR before drawing it up
-   **Esmolol** vial readily available for induction

**Brain Protection Meds:**

-   **Mannitol:** 25% (bags or bottles) available
-   **Mannitol dose:** 1 gram/kg before incision
-   **25% Bottles:** 12-25 grams IV push or
-   **20% Bags:** 0.2 g/mL and can hang to gravity
-   **Lasix** – have available
-   **Decadron** – 10 mg, Check MAR because the patient is usually already on steroids.

-   Infusion pump set-ups:
-   **Nicardipine** (5-15 mg/hour)
-   **Norepinephrine or Neosynephrine**
-   **Remifentanil** (.02-.25 mcg/kg/min as an adjunct to Propofol infusion)
