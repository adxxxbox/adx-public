# Ceftin

### Ceftin (Cefluroxime, Axetil)

Cephalosporin (Second-generation)  
A Beta-Lactam Antibiotic, so avoid with beta lactam allergy.  
Penicillins and Cephalosporins are Beta-Lactams.  
**Dose:** 0.75-1.5 grams  
Popular: 1 gram  
Aerobic Gram positive: weaker than 1st generation.  
Aerobic Gram negative: Stronger than 1st generation.  
**Indications and dosages (PO not included)**  
IV over 3-5 minutes.  
**Disseminated Gonorrhea**  
750mg-1.5 grams IV/IM q8hr  
Ages 12 and older: 750 mg to 1.5 I.V. q 8  
3 months -12 years: 50 to 100 mg/kg/day I.V. or I.M. in divided doses q 6 to 8 hours  
**For Bacterial Meningitis**  
Ages 12 and older: Up to 3 g I.V. or I.M. q 8 hours  
3 months -12 years: 200 to 240 mg/kg I.V. daily in divided doses q 6 to 8 hours.  
**MOA**  
Inhibits mucopeptide synthesis in bacterial cell wall.  
**Renal Impairment**  
CrCl 10-20 mL/min: 750 mg q12hr  
CrCl <10 mL/min: 750 mg q Day  
**Indications**  
Treatment of infections of lower respiratory tract, urinary tract, skin and skin structures, bone and joint; preoperative prophylaxis; treatment of septicemia, gonorrhea, and meningitis caused by susceptible strains of specific microorganisms.  
**Drug Interactions**  
Aminoglycosides  
Increased risk of nephrotoxicity with parenteral cefuroxime.  
**Inhibition of renal excretion of cefuroxime.**  
**Incompatibility:** Aminoglycosides  
Do not add aminoglycosides to cefuroxime solutions because inactivation of both drugs may result; administer at separate sites if concurrent therapy is indicated.  
**Contraindications**  
Hypersensitivity to cephalosporins or penicillins.  
Carnitine deficiency  
**Precautions**  
Use cautiously in: renal or hepatic impairment  
Pregnant or breastfeeding patients  
**Peds**  
**Possible Adverse Reactions**  
**GI:** Nausea; vomiting; diarrhea  
**Genitourinary**  
**Pyuria** renal dysfunction; dysuria; reversible interstitial nephritis; hematuria; toxic nephropathy.  
**Hematologic**  
Eosinophilia neutropenia; lymphocytosis; leukocytosis; thrombocytopenia; decreased platelet function; anemia; aplastic anemia; hemorrhage.  
**Hepatic**  
Hepatic dysfunction; abnormal LFT results.  
**Pregnancy**  
Category B  
Lactation  
Excreted in Breast Milk  
**Overdosage:** possible seizures
