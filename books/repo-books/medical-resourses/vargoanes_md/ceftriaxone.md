### Ceftriaxone

**Ceftriaxone (Rocephin)**  
3rd Generation Cephalosporin  
Semisynthetic and broad-spectrum  
A Beta-Lactam Antibiotic, so avoid with beta lactam allergy.  
Penicillins and Cephalosporins are Beta-Lactams.  
**Doses:**  
The usual adult daily dose is 1 to 2 grams.  
**Popular dose:** 1 gram IV over 30 minutes.  
To be started within 60 minutes prior to incision.  
  
**Peds:**  
Do not give to neonates.  
  
**Infants and children:** 50-100mg/kg/day IV in divided doses Q12-24h.  
**Max:** 4 grams/day  
Moderate activity against aerobic gram-negative bugs.  
Little activity against anaerobic bugs.  
  
**MOA:**  
Same mode of action as other beta-lactam antibiotics: disrupt the synthesis of the peptidoglycan layer of bacterial cell walls.  
**Incompatibilities**  
Vancomycin, amsacrine, aminoglycosides, and fluconazole are physically incompatible with ceftriaxone in admixtures.  
**Literature states NOT to mix with LR or anything containing Ca because particulate formation can result.**  
We still piggy back into IV tubing containing LR w/o issues.  
Don't mix with CaCl or Ca Glutamate.  
  
**Coverage:**  
Improved coverage of Gram negative organisms, except Pseudomonas.  
Reduced Gram positive cover.  
  
**Used for the treatment of:**  
Bacterial Meningitis-choice of drug  
Lower respiratory tract infections  
Acute bacterial Otitus Media  
Skin and skin structure infections  
UTIs  
Uncomplicated gonorrhea (cervical/urethral and rectal)  
  
**Pelvic inflammatory disease:**  
Gonorrhea  
Bacterial septicemia  
Bone and joint infections  
Intra-abdominal infections  
Febrile infants between 4 and 8 weeks of age who are admitted to the hospital to exclude sepsis  
  
**Adverse Reactions:**  
Allergic reactions, drug fever, serum sickness-like reaction, renal dysfunction, toxic nephropathy, reversible hyperactivity, hypertonia, hepatic dysfunction including cholestasis, aplastic anemia, hemorrhage, and super infection.  
  
**Potential Side Effects:**  
**Renal:** Elevations of the BUN (1.2%).  
Flushing was reported occasionally (< 1%).  
  
**Genitourinary:**  
Moniliasis or vaginitis were reported occasionally (< 1%).  
Diarrhea that is watery or bloody  
Fever or chills  
Bruising  
Severe tingling and numbness  
Dark colored urine  
Pain  
Muscle weakness  
Skin (rash, blistering, peeling, white patches or sores inside the mouth or on the lips, pale or yellowed skin)  
  
For the treatment of uncomplicated gonococcal infections, a single intramuscular dose of 250 mg is recommended.
