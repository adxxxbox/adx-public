### One-Lung Ventilation (OLV) Notes

  
_Daniel Perez, CRNA, Kaiser, Orange County California_

**Physiology of one-lung ventilation:**

When one lung it deflated, the blood flow to that lung becomes shunt flow and there is a decease in PaO2.

**Hypoxic pulmonary vasoconstriction (HPV)**

-   Local reaction occurring in hypoxic areas of the lung
-   Vasoconstriction in response to hypoxia in pulmonary arteries
-   Onset and resolution are rapid based on changes in PaO2
-   Triggered by alveolar hypoxia, not arterial hypoxia
-   May be inhibited by calcium channel blockers, volatile agents and vasodilators ​
-   HPV is effective in ⇣ shunt flow (we like HPV with OLV)

**Avoid drugs or events that will inhibit HPV:**

-   Hypervolemia/hypovolemia
-   Excessive tidal volume or PEEP
-   Hypocapnia
-   Hypothermia
-   Acidosis
-   Volatile agents > 1.5 MAC
-   Vasoactive medications
-   Vasodilators (inhibit HPV): Nitroglycerin, Nitroprusside, Dobutamine, Calcium channel blockers, B2 agonists (isoproterenol)
-   Vasoconstrictors (preferentially constrict normally oxygenated pulmonary vessels & reestablish shunt flow):
    -   Dopamine
    -   Epinephrine
    -   Phenylephrine

**Troubleshooting and management for OLV summary:**

Management of OLV:

-   Maintain two-lung ventilation for as long as possible
-   Avoid large tidal volume to avoid barotrauma
-   Add PEEP to those patients without auto-PEEP
-   PIP less than 25 cm H2O
-   Appropriate FiO2 for patient
    -   After ascertaining that oxygenation will be stable after 30 minutes of OLV, the FiO2 should be titrated down to lessen effects of absorptive atelectasis
    -   RR can be adjusted to maintain adequate PaCO2

                      **Causes of Hypoxemia:**

Tube malposition

-   Bronchospasm
-   Decrease CO
-   Hypoventilation
-   Decrease FiO2
-   Pneumothorax of dependent lung
-   Treatment:
    -   **Check tube placement especially every position change with fiberoptic scope**
    -   Increase FIO2 to 100%
    -   CPAP to nondependent, nonventilated lung (start at 2 cm H2O)
    -   PEEP to dependent, ventilated lung

Recruits collapsed airways, Increase compliance, increase FRC

Inform surgeon and go back to two lung ventilation

Ligation of pulmonary artery in pneumonectomy patients

-   At the end of surgery:
    -   Lung separator (DLT clamp, bronchial blocker) should be discontinued & lung inflation with slow breaths achieving a PIP of 30-40 cm H2O
