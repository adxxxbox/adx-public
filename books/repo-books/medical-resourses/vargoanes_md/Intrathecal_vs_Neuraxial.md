### Intrathecal vs Neuraxial

These terms are commonly used in anesthesia textbooks. Here are their definitions:  
  
**Intrathecal Anesthesia or analgesia:** Refers to a “Spinal:” an injection of an anesthetic into the spinal canal, or into the subarachnoid space so that it reaches the cerebrospinal fluid.  
  
**Neuraxial** **Anesthesia:** Refers both to Epidural or Spinal  
The injection of an **anesthetic** in the fatty tissue that surrounds the nerve roots as they exist the spine (epidural) or into the CSF which surrounds the spinal cord (spinal).
