### Ectopic Pregnancy

_Refer to “Ectopic Pregnancy” for a detailed review.  
  
A ruptured ectopic pregnancy is considered a surgical emergency._  
Ultrasound is useful and will show free fluid in the peritoneal cavity.  
Besides abdominal pain, the hemoperitoneum may irritate the phrenic nerve and cause great shoulder pain.  
Some patients may require partial or total salpingectomy.  
It is common for these patients to have greater than 500 mLs of blood in the peritoneal cavity.  
Some may have up to 1,500 mL of blood in the peritoneal cavity.  
  
**Salpingectomy**  
Salpingectomy is indicated when there is uncontrolled bleeding or marked tubal destruction.  
  
**Pre-op:  
Labs:  
**Type & crossmatch  
CBC and BMP  
2 units of PRBC available  
**Anesthetic:** General/ETT with RSI  
**Position:** Lithotomy with Trendelenburg and arms out.  
Supine for open procedure, depending on severity.  
  
**Duration:** 30-90 minutes  
**EBL:** Depends on hemorrhage  
**IV Access:** 1-2 18g  
**Arterial line** : If the patient is unstable.  
**Foley catheter:** Yes  
**Avoid nitrous oxide:** Prevent distension of bowel  
**  
Other drugs to be immediately available:  
**Oxytocin (Pitocin®)  
Carboprost (Hemabate®)  
Methylergonovine (Methergine®)

Acute renal insufficiency**More Notes:  
Hemorrhage:** Within a few hours (usually), the abdomen becomes rigid, and the patient goes into shock.  
Serum pregnancy tests are positive.  
Causes 7% of all pregnancy related deaths.  
Young previously healthy women often maintain a normal BP despite a markedly reduced circulating blood volume.
