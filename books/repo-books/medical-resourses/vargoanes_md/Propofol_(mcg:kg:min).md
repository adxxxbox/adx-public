# Propofol  
10mg/mL in mcg/kg/min

  

<table><tbody><tr><th scope="row">Adult IV<br>Induction Dose</th><td>1.5 - 2.5 mg/kg</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Pediatrics IV<br>Induction Dose</th><td>2.5-3.5 mg/kg</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Minimal sedation</th><td>25 - 100 mcg/kg/min (Titrate to affect)</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">General<br>Anesthesia<br>(TIVA)</th><td>100-300 mcg/kg/min maintenance.</td></tr></tbody></table>

**Patients >60 y.o.** has decreased plasma clearance of propofol  
Elderly requires a lower induction dose (25-50% decrease) because their volume distribution is decreased and a decreased clearance, in contrast to Pediatrics.

* * *

**Children** have a larger volume distribution (50%) and more rapid clearance (25%)

* * *

  
**Equation: (mcg/kg/min)**  
Desired dose x kg x 60 / mcg/mL = mL per hr  
**Example:**  
You desire 20 mcg/kg/min for a 70k pt  
20 x 70 x 60 / 10,000 = mL/hr  
84,000/ 10,000= 8.4 so 8.4 mL/hr = 20 mcg/kg/min
