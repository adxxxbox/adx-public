### Possible Differential Diagnosis of Malignant Hyperthermia (MH)

  
_Edited by Dan Lovinaria, DNP,MS, MBA, APRN, CRNA_

Insufficient Anesthesia  
Inadequate ventilation-low fresh gas flows or dysfunction in the circle  
breathing system.  
Infection or Sepsis  
Iatrogenic overheating  
Serotonin syndrome  
Pheochromocytoma  
Thyrotoxicosis-thyroid storm  
Cocaine or Ecstasy intoxication  
Other muscle disease (dystrphinopathy)  
Neuroleptic malignant syndrome  
Myotonic syndromes  
Cerebral ischemia  
Ascending tonic-clonic syndrome  
Rhabdomyolysis-is the patient taking statins?  
Rhabdomyolysis is associated with patients taking statins.  
The higher the dose of statins, the higher the risk of rhabdomyolysis becomes. The risk also increases if certain drugs — including cyclosporine and gemfibrozil (Lopid) — are taken in combination with statins.  
Exertional heat illness  
  
When one calls the MH Hotline during an event, the consultant will ask you additional questions to try and ascertain if any of the above syndromes may be present.
