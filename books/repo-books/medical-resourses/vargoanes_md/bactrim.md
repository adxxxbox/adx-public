# Bactrim

**Bactrim (TMP/SMX)**  
**Generic name:** trimethoprim/sulfamethoxazole  
The combination of two different antibiotics above.  
Sulfa antibiotic  
**Doses**  
8-10mg/kg/day divided q6-12h  
90-112mg Q6h  
or  
180-225mg Q 12h  
**Indications:**  
Treat and prevent PCP pneumonia  
Ear infections  
Urinary tract infections  
Bronchitis  
Traveler's diarrhea  
Shigellosis  
Pneumocystis pneumonia.  
**Side Effects**  
Fever  
Red, itchy rash  
Nausea / vomiting  
Low neutrophil count (neutropenia)  
Low platelet count (thrombocytopenia)  
Peeling or sloughing of skin  
**FDA pregnancy category C.**  
It is not known whether sulfamethoxazole and trimethoprim will harm an unborn baby.  
Bactrum can pass into breast milk and may harm a nursing baby. Do not give this medication to a child younger than 2 months old. Older adults may be more likely to have side effects from this antibiotic.
