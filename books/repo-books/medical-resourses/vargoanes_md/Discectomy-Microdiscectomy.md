### Discectomy or Microdiscectomy

**Procedure:** To remove part or all of a bulging or damaged (herniated) disc in the lower spine.

A laminectomy is needed if the surgeon has to cut a window through the vertebrae to get access to the disk.

It depends on the location of the herniation.

The C-arm is first used to verify the correct disc and point of incision.

**Microdiscectomy** just means the surgeon uses a large microscope to perform the discectomy.

**The incision is minimal:** 1-2 inches

**Indications:**

**Sciatica or lumbar radiculopathy:** When the disc presses on the spinal nerves and causes pain and numbness in the buttock and leg.

**Anesthetic:** General/ETT

**Muscle Relaxation:** Usually, check with the surgeon.

**Duration:** 30-60 minutes

**EBL:** Minimal

**IV Access:** 20G is acceptable.

-   Ensure the IV site is not inserted at an AC site.
-   It may kink and/or run slow when the arms are flexed 90 degrees in the prone position.

**Antibiotics:** Usually Ancef, unless contraindicated.

**Neuro Monitoring:** No

**Position:** Prone

**Positioning note** Using an Andrews Table with the patient in knees-to-chest positioning is no longer utilized.

-   Before you log roll the patient prone onto the OR table, ensure you have a foam cradle or a Prone View in place on the patient’s face.
-   Most anesthesia providers place eye goggles over the patient’s eyes.
-   A plastic flex accordion extender for the ETT is commonly used.

**After rolling the patient to prone position:**

-   Reconnect the ventilator tubing and monitors.
-   Re-auscultate for bilateral breath sounds to verify ETT has not moved out of position.
-   Check eyes, ears, and nose.
-   Ensure the patient’s neck is in a neutral position (neither overextended nor flexed).
-   Make sure shoulders are not over extended (if arms are out).
-   The ETT should be free without kinking or undue traction.
-   You should be able to visually see and have access to tubing connections.
-   Make sure the chest rolls are in their proper place, especially for well-endowed females, and the shoulders are padded.

**Possible Complications:**

-   A recurrent disc herniation
-   Dural tear with cerebrospinal fluid leak: This occurs in 1-2% of these surgeries. It does not change the results of surgery, but post-operatively, the patient may be asked to lay recumbent for one to two days to allow the leak to seal.
-   Nerve root damage
-   Bowel/bladder incontinence
-   Bleeding
-   Infection
