Yes, the formula on your page is the standard MABL expression and it is correct as written.  

## MABL formula

Most anesthesia and perioperative references define allowable or maximum allowable blood loss as:  
\[
\text{ABL} = \text{EBV} \times \frac{H_{i} - H_{f}}{H_{i}}
\]
where \(H_{i}\) is the initial hematocrit and \(H_{f}\) is the minimum acceptable hematocrit.[1][2][3]

Your page’s formula  
\[
\text{ABL} = \text{EBV} \times \frac{\text{current HCT} - \text{accepted HCT}}{\text{current HCT}}
\]
is algebraically identical to this (\(\text{current HCT} = H_{i}\), \(\text{accepted HCT} = H_{f}\)), so it is correct.[2][3][1]

## EBV values

The EBV ranges you listed (premature 90–100 mL/kg, term neonate 80–90 mL/kg, infant 70–80 mL/kg, child ~70 mL/kg, adult ~55–60 mL/kg) are within the typical ranges given in pediatric and general references, which describe EBV as approximately 90–100 mL/kg in preterm neonates, 80–90 mL/kg in term neonates, 70–80 mL/kg in infants, 70–75 mL/kg in children, and about 65–70 mL/kg in adults.[4][5][6][7]
