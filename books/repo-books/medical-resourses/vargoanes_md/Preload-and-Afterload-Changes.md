### Preload and Afterload Changes

**ESV-End Systolic Volume  
EDV-End Diastolic Volume**

**Preload**     
Pertains to LV function and volumes  
Normal EDV-150ml, SV 75ml; so if SV is decreased, then BP also decreases.

**If you increase Preload by giving fluids, this**:  
Increases EDV (filling)  
No changes in ESV  
Increases SV  
Increases BP  
Decrease HR reflexively via Starling’s Law of constant Cardiac Output.

**If you decrease preload using Lasix, or NTG, then the results are:**  
Decreases in EDV  
No changes in ESV  
Decreases in SV  
Decreases in BP  
Increases HR-(reflex)

**Afterload**  
If you increase Afterload for instance, by giving Neo, the results are:  
Increases in BP.  
Decreases in SV.  
Increases in ESV (LV volumes).

If you decrease Afterload by using Nipride for example, the results are:  
Decreases in BP due to a decrease in the SVR.  
Increases SV.  
Decreases ESV (LV volumes).  
Decreases PCWP. PCWP preload: Lasix and NTG will decrease PCWP.
