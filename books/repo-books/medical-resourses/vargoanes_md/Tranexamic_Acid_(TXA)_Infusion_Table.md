### Tranexamic Acid (TXA) 1 mg/kg/hr Infusion Table

A common Tranexamic acid infusion protocol for certain procedures (mostly orthopedic) is 1 mg/kg/hr.  
  
For high-dose TXA, the loading dose is 50mg/kg infused over 15 minutes, followed by a maintenance dose of 20mg/kg/hr.  
  
**Preparation:**  
Remove 10 mLs of saline from a 100 mL IV bag of normal saline (NS).  
Inject 1 gram (10 mLs from a 1-gram vial of TXA) into the 100 mL IV bag of NS.  
This results in a concentration of the bag being 10 mg/mL of TXA.

**Cardiac surgery infusion rate of TXA:**  
After induction of anesthesia and prior to skin incision, administer a pre-surgical loading dose of 15 mg/kg TRANEXAMIC ACID LINK, followed by infusion of 4.5 mg/kg/h for the duration of surgery.  
0.6 mg/kg of this elder dose may be added to the priming volume of the heart-lung machine.
