# Desflurane

**Desflurane (Suprane)**  
A fluorinated methyl ether ether.  
The fastest inhalation agent we use.  
**Color Code:** Black  
**MAC:**(30-55yo): 6.6%  
**MAC:** (>60 yo): 5-6%  
**Peds MAC:** 8-9%  
**Blood:Gas Partition Coefficient:** 0.42.  
**Vapor Pressure:** 669  
Poor blood solubility but extremely fast onset/offset.  
Very Pungent-produces airway irritation, salivation, breath holding, coughing or laryngospasm when >6% is delivered to an awake patient.  
Some literature states that the vapor pressure is so high that it would boil at normal operating room pressures.  
Requires a special electrical heated pressurized vaporizer.  
**Degrades to CO with Soda Lime:** Yes, the highest amount compared to Iso and Sevo.  
The most is expensive inhalation agent.  
Stimulates catecholamine release possibly increases post operative nausea and vomiting.  
Desflurane differs from Isoflurane only be substitution of a fluorine atom for the chlorine atom found on the alpha-ethyl component of isoflurane.  
**Contraindicated:** Inhalation induction or reactive airway disease.
