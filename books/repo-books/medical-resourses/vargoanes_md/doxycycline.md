# Doxycycline

**Doxycycline (Vibromycin)**  
Virtually the same as Tetracycline.  
However, Tetracycline is only PO.  
**Brand names:** Vibromycin, Adoxa, Alodox, Avidoxy, Doryx, Monodox, Morgidox, Ocudox, Oracea, Oraxyl  
A broad spectrum synthetic Tetracycline antibiotic  
**Doses:**  
Standard dose:100 or 200mg IV  
Doxycycline should be used only to treat or prevent infections that are proven or strongly suspected to be caused by bacteria.  
**Peds:**  
Children>8 years: 2.2-4.4mg/kg/day in divided doses Q12-24h  
**Max:** 200mg/day  
**MOA**  
Inhibits of protein synthesis  
**FDA pregnancy category D**  
Do not give to a pregnant patient. It could harm the unborn baby or cause permanent tooth discoloration later in life.  
Doxycycline can make birth control pills less effective.  
**Avoid with Kids**  
Children should not use this medicine. Doxycycline can cause permanent yellowing or graying of the teeth in children younger than 8 years old.  
**Side Effects** Diarrhea  
Itching of the rectum or vagina  
Sore mouth  
If you have diabetes, doxycycline can cause false results in some tests for sugar in the urine.  
**Indicated for:**  
Syphilis  
Chlamydial  
Lyme Disease  
Mycoplasma infections  
Acne  
Rickettsia infections  
Malaria: Malaria is caused by a protist and not a bacterium.  
Prophylaxis of anthrax.  
Effective against Bubonic plaque.
