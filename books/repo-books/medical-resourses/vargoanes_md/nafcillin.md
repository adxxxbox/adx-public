# Nafcillin

**Nafcillin (Nallpen, Unipen)**  
**Class:** Penicillin antibiotic  
Narrow-spectrum, beta-lactam antibiotic  
A Beta-Lactam Antibiotic, so avoid with beta lactam allergy  
Antistaphylococcal Penicillin  
**Doses:**  
0.5-2 grams IV/IM  
2 grams Q4h or 3 grams Q6h  
**Peds:** (Infant and Children)  
50-200mg/kg/day  
**Indication:**  
Staph Aureus and Staph Epidemidis.  
NOT effective against MRSA and MRSE.  
**Side-effects:**  
Nausea and vomiting  
Diarrhea, often due to suppression of normal gastrointestinal bacteria, which, on occasion, leads to a more serious super-infection with an organism like Clostridium difficile.  
Abdominal pain  
Yeast infections (thrush) affecting the mouth and tongue or vagina.  
Agranulocytosis, neutropenia
