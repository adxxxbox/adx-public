| **Ibuprofen (Motrin, Advil, Rufin, Neoprofen, Caldolor):**  
**Note:** Caldolor is IV  
**Dose: 1 gram** PO (Do NOT exceed 2,400 mg of ibuprofen in a 24-hour period)  
600-800 mg Ibuprofen (4 Advil or Motrin)  
**MOA:** COX inhibition (nonselective)  
  
**Exercise caution in patients with:**  
GI ulcers  
Chronic kidney disease  
Coagulopathy  
  
**Celebrex (Celecoxib):**  
**Dose:** 400 mg PO at least two hours preoperatively.  
**MOA:** COX-2 selective inhibition  
It decreases the risk of a GI ulcer compared with nonselective COX inhibitor (Ibuprofen)  
Thus, Celebrex can be substituted for Ibuprofen to reduce this risk.  
  
**Concerns:**  
Increases the risk for MI.  
Avoid with CAD  
Avoid in CHF  
Avoid with in patients with sulfa allergies.  
Celebrex is a diaryl-substituted pyrazole derivative containing a sulfonamide substituent.  
  
**Meloxicam** (Mobic, Vivlodex, Mobicox)  
**Dose:** 15 mg PO (max dose 15 mg/day)  
**MOA:** A newer COX-2 inhibitor  
Caution in bleeding disorder patients, cardiac disease and renal disease. |

| **Acetaminophen** (Tylenol, Panadol, Tempra, Ofirmiv, paracetamol, Abenol, Atasol, Perditeix)  
**Dose:** 1-1.5 grams PO 2 hours prior to surgery  
The maximum recommended daily dose for ≥ 50 kg: 4 grams  
  
**MOA:** Centrally acting analgesic; possible COX inhibition.  
**Note:** Ofirmiv is IV  
Caution in liver failure patients  
Acetaminophen is non-aspirin pain reliever |

| **Gabapentinoids:** These are anti-epileptic medications used to treat seizures and nerve pain (neuropathic pain).  
**MOA:** Inhibition of voltage-gated calcium channels  
**Pregabalin (Lyrica):**  
**Dose:** 150-300 mg/day oral (2.5 mg/kg) 1-2 hours preoperatively  
Lyrica is absorbed faster with a faster onset than gabapentin.  
Lyrica reaches its peak concentration within an hour of being taken, whereas it takes 3 to 4 hours for gabapentin to reach its peak concentration.  
Other uses for Lyrica are neuropathic (nerve) pain associated with diabetic peripheral neuropathy or postherpetic neuralgia and fibromyalgia.  
(The DEA has classified Lyrica as a controlled substance (listed in Schedule V).  
  
**Concerns:**  
Can cause sedation in elderly  
Reduce the dose in patients with reduced CrCl  
  
**Gabapentin (Neurontin):**  
600-1,200 mg 1-2 hours preoperatively  
Caution with increased abuse potential and respiratory depression  
Avoided in patients > 65 years of age and those who have sleep-disordered breathing (eg, obstructive sleep apnea).  
Gabapentin is also used to treat nerve pain caused by shingles (herpes zoster).  
**Concerns:**  
Can cause sedation in elderly  
Reduce the dose in patients with reduced CrCl |
