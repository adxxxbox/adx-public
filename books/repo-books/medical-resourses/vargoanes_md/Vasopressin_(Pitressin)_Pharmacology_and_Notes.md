# Vasopressin (Pitressin) Pharmacology and Notes

Vasopressin (arginine vasopressin, AVP; antidiuretic hormone, ADH)  
Hormone formed in the hypothalamus and released from the posterior pituitary. Main function is to regulate extracellular fluid (anti-diuretic) via reabsorption in renal tubules.  
Also potent vasoconstrictor-Arterial, mesenteric.

* * *

**Classification  
**Pituitary - antidiuretic agent  

* * *

**  
Indications  
**Diabetes Insipidus  
GI Bleed (control of bleeding esophageal varices- mesenteric vasoconstriction)  
Septic Shock-when Levophed, Dobutamine Dopamine are not effective.

* * *

**POTENTIAL HAZARDS  
**thrombophlebitis Infusion may result in reduced cardiac output, angina, arrythmias and cardiac arrest.Always have nitroglycerine ready in conjunction with vasopressin to reduce cardiovascular complications.Generalized vasoconstriction - cramping, bowel necrosis, lower limb ischemia, myocardial infarction.

* * *

**IMPORTANT IMPLICATIONS  
**Local arterial bleeding, hepatic, gastric and intestinal infarcts have been reported.Hyponatremia with water retention; water intoxication.  
Caution in patients with asthma, epilepsy, migraine, heart failure, and other conditions which may be aggravated by water intoxication.  
Rapid rebound hypotension as a common problem when infusion is stopped.

* * *

**Method of Action**

Arterial constriction  
Mesenteric vasoconstriction  
H2O reabsorption in renal tubules

* * *

**Onset  
**10-15minsutes.Because vasopressin is a hormone and not a receptor agonist, it typically takes 10-15 minutes to elicit a clinical response.

* * *

**Complications**  
Avoid patients with significant vascular disease esp. AD; cardiac ischemia, peripheral cyanosis or Ischemia, arrhythmias, oliguria, hyponatremia, H2O intoxication, tremor, vomiting, abdominal cramps.  
Vasopressin should be given to a pregnant woman only if clearly needed.

* * *

**How it controls GI Bleed:**  
Causes contraction of smooth muscle of the gastrointestinal tract and of all parts of the vascular bed.

* * *

**Metabolized  
**Majority rapidly destroyed by liver and kidneys.  

* * *

  
**Half Life  
**10-20 mins
