# Bumex

**Bumetanide (Bumex)**  
**Supplied**  
25 mg(100ml)/ empty viaflex(IV bag)  
2.5mg/ 10 ml vial.  
May also be given undiluted  
**CLINICAL PHARMACOLOGY**  
Bumex -loop diuretic with rapid onset and short duration.  
Inhibits reabsorbtion of Na and Cl in the ascending loop of Henle and proximal renal tubule.  
**INDICATIONS AND USAGE**  
Edema associated with CHF, hepatic and renal disease, including the nephrotic syndrome.  
Almost equal diuretic response occurs after oral and IV. Can also give IM  
Loop Diuretic-just like Lasix (Non potassium sparing)  
**Use:** diuretic  
**Dose:** 0.5-1mg/dose IV push over 1-2 mins; maximum 10mg/day.  
**Usual infusion rates:** 0.1 to 1 mg/hr.  
**Adverse effects:** hyperurecemia, hypochloremia, hypokalemia, and azotemia.  
**CONTRAINDICATIONS**  
Contraindicated in anuria.  
Although Bumex can be used to induce diuresis in renal insufficiency, any marked increase in BUN or creatinine, or the development of oliguria during therapy of patients with progressive renal disease, is an indication for discontinuation of treatment.  
Also contraindicated in patients in hepatic coma or in states of severe electrolyte depletion until the condition is improved or corrected.  
Also contraindicated in patients hypersensitive to this drug  
**OVERDOSAGE**  
Overdosage can lead to acute profound water loss, volume and electrolyte depletion, dehydration, reduction of blood volume and circulatory collapse with a possibility of vascular thrombosis and embolism. Electrolyte depletion may be manifested by weakness, dizziness, mental confusion, anorexia, lethargy, vomiting and cramps.  
Treatment of overdose consists of replacement of fluid and electrolyte losses by careful monitoring of the urine and electrolyte output and serum electrolyte levels.  
**Supplied:** 2.5mg/ 10 ml vial.  
**Equivalent dosages:**  
1 mg Bumex =40mg Lasix = 10 to 20mg Demadex (torsemide)  
Higher doses are required in renal failure (usually > 10 mg/day).
