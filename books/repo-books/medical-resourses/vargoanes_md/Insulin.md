### Insulin

**Insulin**  
Secreted by the Beta cells of the islets of Langerhans of the Pancreas in response to a rising glucose level. Then lowers the blood sugar level permitting glucose to enter the cells.  
Pancreas secretes around 40 units/day.  
**Class:** Hormone (hypoglycemic agent).  
**Actions:** Causes uptake of glucose by the cells, decreases blood glucose level, promotes glucose storage.  
**Insulin Doses (Regular)**  
**For Hyperkalemia**  
5-10 units of IVP given with 25-50 grams of Dextrose or 1-2 amps of D50 (drop the % and add or 1-2 amps of D50).  
**For Hyperglycemia**  
Load 0.1 unit/kg IVP  
Maintenance 0.1 units/kg/hr, titrate to desired glucose.  
**Basic Operating Room Insulin Sliding Scale**  
**Goal:** Keep below 150  
**Glucose Levels:**  
**<110:** No action  
**110-150:** Units repeat blood sugar in 1 hour  
**150-200:**6 units repeat blood sugar in 1 hour  
**200-250:** 10 units repeat blood sugar in 1 hour  
**\>250:**20 units repeat blood sugar in 1 hour  
Mix 100 units in 100 D5W or NS (1:1 ratio).  
If you want 6 units per hr, just set your pump on 6mL/hr.  
  
**Indications:** Elevated blood glucose, diabetic ketoacidosis.  
**Contraindications:** Avoid over compensation of blood glucose level; if possible. Not having glucometer available.  
**Precautions:** Administration of excessive dose may induce hypoglycemia. Glucose and glucometer should be available.  
**Side Effects:** Few in emergency situations.  
**Routes:** IV, SQ. **Pediatric Dosage:** Dosage is based on blood glucose level.
