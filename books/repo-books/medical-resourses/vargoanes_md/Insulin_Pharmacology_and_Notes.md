# Insulin Pharmacology and Notes

Secreted by the Beta cells of the islets of Langerhans of the Pancrease in response to a rising glucose level. Then lowers the blood sugar level permitting glucose to enter the cells.

* * *

**Class:** Hormone (hypoglycemic agent).  
  

* * *

**Actions:** Causes uptake of glucose by the cells, decreases blood glucose level, promotes glucose storage.  
  

* * *

**Indications:** Elevated blood glucose, diabetic ketoacidosis.  
  

* * *

**Contraindications:** Avoid over compensation of blood glucose level; if possible . Not having glucometer available  
  

* * *

**Precautions:** Administration of excessive dose may induce hypoglycemia. Glucose and glucometer should be available.  
  

* * *

**Side Effects:** Few in emergency situations.  
**Routes:** IV, SQ.  
**Pediatric Dosage:** Dosage is based on blood glucose level.

* * *

**Types of insulin we administer:**

**Rapid-acting**: Humalog® (lispro), Novolog® (aspart), Apidra® (glulisine) Onset 5-15 minutes  
Peak 30 - 90 minutes,  
Duration 3-6 hours  
  

* * *

**Short Acting Insulin:** Humulin® R, Novolin® R  
Onset 30 mins  
Peak 2-3 hours  
Duration 3-6 hours

* * *

**Intermediate- Acting Insulin**: Humalin® N (NPH), Novolin® N (NPH)  
Onset 2-4 hours  
Peak 4-12 hours  
Duration 12-18 hours
