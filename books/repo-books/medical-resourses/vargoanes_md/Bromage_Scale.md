### Bromage Scale

Bromage grade 3 = patient is unable to perform a straight leg raise  
  
This means the spinal anesthetic block has reached the high lumbar segment.  
  
The loss of afferent - sensory-pin prick should be higher and assessed up to the T-4 (nipple area).

| Grade | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | No motor block |
| 1 | Can move knees/feet, but can't raise the leg |
| 2 | Can move feet, can't raise knee and leg |
| 3 | Complete block of motor limb |
