### Postpartum Hemorrhage Uterotonic Agents and Doses

**Immediate medications to consider for postpartum hemorrhage**

| Drug | Dosing |
| --- | --- |
| **Pitocin**  
(oxytocin) | First line agent  
10 to 40 units in a 500 to 1,000 mL NS infusion or  
10 units IM |
| **Tranexamic Acid:**  
(Cyklokapron®) | 1 gram IV over 10 minutes (add to 100ml NS) may give a  
2nd 1 gram dose 30 minutes after the last dose. |
| **Methergine®  
**(Methylergonovine) | 0.2 mg IM every 2 to 4 hours |
| **Hemabate®**  
(Carboprost) | 0.25 mg IM every 15 to 90 minutes up to 8 doses or  
500 mcg IM incrementally up to 3 mg or  
0.5 mg intramyometrial |
| **Cytotec®**  
(misoprostol)  
A prostaglandin E1 analogue | Use only when oxytocin is not available.  
600 mcg to 1 mg rectally  
800 mcg oral or sublingual  
Equivalent to 40 IU of IV oxytocin in women who  
have received oxytocin for the prevention of PPH in the 3rd stage of labor.  
Contraindicated in asthmatics |
| **Dinoprostone**  
(prostaglandin E2) | 20 mg vaginally or rectally  
Rarely readily available. |
| **Recombinant human factor VIIa**  
(rFVIIa)  
NovoSeven® | 50 to 100 mcc/kg Q2 hours  
has been effectively used "off label" on an empirical basis in the  
treatment of massive PPH which did not respond to conventional method  
Rarely readily available. |
