| Hemodynamic alteration | Peak time of effect | Potential risk |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cardiac output ↑30 to 50% | 20 to 24 weeks | ↑ risk for CHF in women with limited cardiac function |
| Stoke volume ↑ 20% | 20 to 24 weeks | ↑ preload with an obstructive lesion (mitral or aortic stenosis) LV dysfunction |
| HR ↑ 10 to 20% | 3rd trimester | Tachycardia causes palpitations; impairs ventricular filling |
| Blood volume ↑40% | 20 to 24 weeks | Physiologic anemia caused by less increased in erythrocyte mass |
| Peripheral vasodilation | Throughout | ↓ BP; ↓ valvular regurgitation |

**HR:** ↑ 10 to 20%  
**SV:** ↑ 10%

**Cardiovascular Physiology with vaginal delivery:  
**Initial **↑** blood volume by ~500 mL by the displacement of blood during uterine contraction followed by a loss of 10% blood volume.  
**Cardiovascular Physiology with C-section:  
Blood loss:** ~ 30% of blood volume**BP, CO and HR with contractions:** **Increase
