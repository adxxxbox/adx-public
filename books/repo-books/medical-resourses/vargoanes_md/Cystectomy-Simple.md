### Cystectomy- Simple

Removal of the entire bladder without the removal of adjacent structures or organs. However, a urinary diversion is then created. So 1-2 more hours are now needed.  
A Lower abdominal incision is made.  
**Frequency of Occurrence:** Male:Female 3:1  
**Male:** The surgeon leaves behind the prostate, urethra, and seminal vesicles with the advantage that potency is conserved.   
**Female:** They leave behind the urethra, uterus, and anterior wall of the vagina.   
**Anesthetic:** General/ETT  
If Spinal-T4 sensory level. Use epi in the SAB because these can be long cases.  
**Duration:** 4 hours  
**EBL:** Depends on the surgeon. 250-1,000mLs  
**Type and Cross: Yes**  
**Epidural:** Sometimes for post op pain.  
**A-line:** Sometimes, not as common as before.  
**IV Access:** 2 18G or a 16G in case of increased EBL.  
**Position:** Supine, one or both arms out.  
  
**Possible Complications**  
Hemorrhage  
Wound Infection  
UTI  
DVT  
Ureterointestinal leakage  
Ileus
