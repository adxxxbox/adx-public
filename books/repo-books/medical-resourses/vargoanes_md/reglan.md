# Reglan

**Reglan (Metoclopramide)**  
Methoxychloroprocainamide  
**Class:** Antiemetic/GI Prokinetic.  
Gastrokinetic Agent and Dopamine receptor antagonist that stimulates GI motility, increases gastroesophageal sphincter tone, relaxes pylorus & duodenum.  
We mostly use Reglan in Pre-op for gastric aspiration prophylaxis in high risk patients.  
It USED to be the prokenetic drug of choice to give in Holding to assure an empty stomach for induction.  
Not as popular as before due to the extrapyrimadal side effects.  
**Doses:**  
Standard is 10mg IV  
0.1-0.25mg/kg IV q6-8hr  
5-20mg IV  
Pre-operative disease in Gastric Fluid Disease: 10-20mg over 3-5 mins  
**Elderly:** 5 mg  
**Onset:** < 30 minutes  
**Peak:** 1-2 hours  
**Duration:** 1-2 hours  
**MOA:**  
Dopamine -receptor antagonists, even in the CNS. So it may cause extrapyramidal side effects.  
Produces selective cholinergic stimulation of the upper GI tract.  
Increases LES tone.  
Decreases gastric emptying time.  
Also antagonizes the dopamine receptors on the chemoreceptor trigger zone.  
Recommended 15-30 minutes before induction.  
Usually given with 20mg Pepcid.  
**Indications:**  
Treatment for gastroparesis.  
GERD  
Full stomach before emergency case.  
**Contraindications:**  
Parkinson's  
Seizure disorder  
GI obstruction  
**Note:**  
However, there was a study (displayed in Stoelting) on gastric emptying time using radioactive labeled chicken liver, and beef stew on a diabetic patient.  
**The study showed:** With and without Reglan, there was no real difference in the volume remaining in the stomach the first 20 minutes. A significant decrease with the Reglan patients after 100 minutes.
