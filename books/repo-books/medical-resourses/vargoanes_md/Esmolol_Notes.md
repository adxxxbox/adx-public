### Esmolol (Brevibloc) Pharmacology and Notes

**Esmolol (Brevibloc) summary of infusion considerations.**  
  
**Common dilution:** 2.5 Grams in 250ml of NS or D5W = 10,000 mcg/ml (10mg/ml).  
  
**Infusion Dose Range:** 50-250 mcg/kg/min.  
Adjust infusion rate in increments of 25-50 mcg/kg/min.  
  
**For SVT, intraoperative tachycardia, and/or hypertension**  
IV: Initial bolus: 0.5-1 mg/kg over 1 minute, followed by a 150 mcg/kg/minute infusion.  
  
**For control of postoperative hypertension**  
Titrate for desired heart rate and/or blood pressure up to 250-300 mcg/kg/minute as needed.  
  
**Method of Action:** Selective Beta-1 Blocker  
**Onset of action:** 2-10 minutes ( most rapid when loading doses are administered).  
  
**Half-life:** 10 minutes.  
Duration of hemodynamic effects: 10-30 minutes, prolonged following higher cumulative doses, or extended duration of use.  
Half-life elimination: Adults: 10 minutes, longer with Renal Disease  
  
**Adverse reactions**  
Severe bradycardia  
Dose-related hypotension  
Ventricular arrhythmias  
Heart failure  
  
**Direct Contraindications**  
Sinus bradycardia  
First-degree heart block  
Overt cardiac failure
