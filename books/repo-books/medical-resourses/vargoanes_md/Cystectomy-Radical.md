### Cystectomy (Radical)

The removal of the entire bladder, nearby lymph nodes (lymphadenectomy), part of the urethra, and nearby organs that may contain cancer cells.  
**Indications:** To treat cancer of the bladder, radiation cystitis, neurologic disorders, congenital defects and intractable infections.  
**Male:** The prostate, the seminal vesicles, and part of the vas deferens are also removed.  
**Female:** The cervix, the uterus, the ovaries, the fallopian tubes, and part of the vagina are also removed. **A urinary diversion is then created.**

**Frequency of Occurrence:** Male : Female 3:1  
**Anesthetic:** General/ETT, epidural for post op pain at T4 level.  
**Position:** Supine; some surgeons will place females in lithotomy.  
\[While the urethra and bladder are mobilized, male patients are supine and female patients are lithotomy.\]  
**Duration:** 4-6h  
**EBL:** 400-1,500 (depends on the surgeon)  
**Type/Screened:** yes  
**Blood on stand-by:** yes, 2 units  
**A-line:** Recommended  
**IV Access:** 2 18G or a 16G in case of increased EBL.

**Possible Complications**  
Hemorrhage  
Wound Infection  
UTI  
DVT  
Ureterointestinal leakage  
Ileus
