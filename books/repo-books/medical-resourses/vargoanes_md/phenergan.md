# Phenergan

**Phenergan (Promethazine)**  
**Class:** Phenothiazine (same as Thorazine)  
H1 blocker  
Antimuscarinic action at CTZ.  
Antiemetic of choice when Zofran and Decadron does not work.  
Also a strong sedative - be careful, it could prolong emergence.  
**Doses:** (usually in a 25mg vial)  
**Popular dose:** 6.25-12.5mg IV  
**Allergic reaction:** 25mg  
**Suppository:** 12.5mg, 25mg or 50mg  
12.5-25mg IM  
25mg PO  
**Geriatric:** Lower dose: 6.25mg IV  
**Peds:**  
\>2 yo  
**Peds for Nausea:** 0.25-1 mg/kg PO/IM/IV q4-6h prn.  
**Max:** 25 mg/dose  
**Peds Sedation:** 12.5-25 mg PO/IM/IV x1;  
**Max:** 25 mg/dose;  
**Literature states:** IM preferred over IV; use lowest effective dose; avoid other resp. depressants.  
Don't use below age 2 due to potential fatal respiratory .  
**IV onset:** 3-5 minutes  
**IM onset:** 20 minutes  
**Duration:** 4-6 hours, may persist 12 hours  
**Metabolized:** Liver  
**Effects Phenergan Possesses**  
Anti Histamine H1  
Anti-muscarinic  
Sedative  
Anti-motion sickness  
Anti-emetic  
Anti-cholinergic  
**Indications:**  
Allergic Reactions as secondary alternative to Benadryl.  
Sedation-relief of apprehension.  
Anti-emetic  
Prevent/treat Motion sickness  
Adjunct to analgesia/anesthesia-reducing narcotic needs.  
If patient is awake-give slowly-it burns.  
**Warning:**  
**Seizure Disorder:** lowers threshold.  
Contains Metabisulfite- a sulfite that may cause allergic-type reactions.  
**Respiratory depression:** amplified by concomitant of narcotics.  
CNS Depression-amplified by use of anesthesia  
Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome- been reported in association with promethazine HCl alone or in combination with antipsychotic drugs  
**Epinephrine:** it can reverse Epi's vasopressor effect, Epi should NOT be used to treat hypotension associated with PHENERGAN Injection overdose.  
Infiltration-terrible tissue damage can cause gangrene.  
**Contraindications:**  
Coma  
Hypersensitivity  
Open or closed angle glaucoma  
Acute bronchospam (thickens bronchial secretions).  
Signs associated with Reye's Syndrome.  
**Drug Interactions:**  
Additive effects with other CNS depressants & anticholinergics. MAOIs increase the incidence of EPS.
