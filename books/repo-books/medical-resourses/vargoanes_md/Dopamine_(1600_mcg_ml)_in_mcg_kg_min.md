# Dopamine 1,600mcg/ml in mcg/kg/min

<table><tbody><tr><td><b>Refractory CHF</b><br>initial dose: 0.5 to 2 mcg/kg/min</td></tr><tr><td><b>For Renal</b><br>(1-5 mcg/kg/min) increases urine output per dopamine receptors</td></tr><tr><td><b>For Cardiac</b><br>(2-10 mcg/kg/min) increases systolic and pulse pressure via myocardial contractility (usually start at 5mcg/kg/min) via beta 1 receptors</td></tr><tr><td><b>For Cardiac and Vasocontriction</b><br>(10-20mcg/kg/min)</td></tr><tr><td><b>Cardiac life support (initial):</b><br>2 to 5 mcg/kg/min - titrate to effect. Increase by 1-4 mcg/kg/minute at 10 to 30 minute intervals. If dosages &gt;20-30 mcg/kg/minute are needed -consider a levophed or Epi drip a more direct-acting pressor may be more beneficial [0.5 to 2 mcg/kg/min-dopa; 2-10-dopa/beta; &gt;10-primarily alpha.]</td></tr></tbody></table>

  
**Equation**  
400mg/250ml Bag (1.6mg/ml or 1,600mcg/ml)  
Desired Dose x 60 x kg / 1,600= ml/hr

* * *

**Example:**  
Wanting 5mcg/kg/min for a 30kg pt  
5 x 60 x 30 / 1,600 =  
9,000/1,600 = 5.6 ml/hr
