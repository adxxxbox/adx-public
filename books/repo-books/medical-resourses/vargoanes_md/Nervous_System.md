### Nervous System

**Nervous System  
**There is an increase in cerebral blood flow during pregnancy.  
The increase is secondary to the decrease in cerebral vascular resistance and an increase in internal carotid diameter.  
  
**Changes in the brain  
**Increased permeability of the blood brain barrier leads to an increased hydrostatic pressure.  
Increase in capillary density in the posterior cerebral cortex.  
  
**Pregnancy Associated Sleep Disorder**  
Pregnant women have more insomnia and daytime sleepiness.  
Progesterone has a strong sedative effect.  
Increased cortisol is associated with increased REM sleep.  
Sleep duration is shorter in pregnancy.Poor sleep quality increases as pregnancy progresses.  
23% experience restless leg syndrome in the third trimester.  
Sleep may be poor up to 3 months post-partum.
