# Fentanyl (Sublimaze) Pharmacology and Notes

Most common narcotic used in Anesthesia.

Fentanyl was first synthesized by Dr. Paul Janssen in 1960.

100 mcg is equivalent to 10mg of morphine and 75mg of Demerol.

It is synthetic, causes less nausea than other narcotics.

* * *

**Fentanyl** (1-2mcg/kg per dose initially)

**Class:** Opioid analgesic

**Half-life:** 2-4 hours

Duration of action of analgseia after 100mcg bolus (2mLs)-30-60mins

**Metabolism:** By liver, excreted by kidney (<10%)

**Precautions:** May cause chest wall rigidity in neonates at high doses.

**Onset of action:** almost immediate maximal analgesic and respiratory depressant effect may not be noted for several minutes 100 times more potent than morphine as an analgesic It is a mu opioid receptor agonist with high lipid solubility and a rapid onset and short duration of effects.  
Rapidly crosses the blood-brain barrier Causes more muscle rigidity than other opioids. Does not cause histamine release and has minimal depressant effects on the heart.

* * *

**Common analogues of fentanyl:**  
Alfentanil (Alfenta)  
Sufentanil (Sufenta)  
Remifentanil (Ultiva)
