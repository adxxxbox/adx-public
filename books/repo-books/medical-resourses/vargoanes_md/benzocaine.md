# Benzocaine

**Benzocaine (Auralgan) - Topical**  
**Class:** Ester type Local Anesthetic, Fast Onset, Short Acting  
Topical anesthesia for mucus membranes / GI .  
**Goal:** Gag reflex suppression, and anorectal disorders and various pain syndromes.  
**Available:** Gels, creams, ointments, lotions, aerosols, and lozenges. Many nonprescription products are available for the temporary relief of dental or oral pain.  
Minimally absorbed - relatively free from systemic side effects or adverse drug interactions.  
**Benzocaine (Auralgan)**  
**Topical up to 20%**  
**Onset:** Fast  
**Duration:** 30-60 mins  
**Max Dose:** 200mg  
**MOA:** Like other local anesthetics, it binds to the intracellular portion of sodium channels and blocks sodium influx into nerve cells, which prevents depolarization.  
**In Detail:** Prevents transmission of nerve impulses by inhibiting the passage of Na ions through their specific channels. It is speculated that local anesthetics bind to specific sites on the inner portion of the Na channels (internal gate or H gate) near their external openings to maintain those channels in an inactivated state.
