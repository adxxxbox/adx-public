### Tourniquet Specifications

**Bruner’s ten rules for the safe use of a tourniquet (Modified by Barithwaite and Klenerman)**

Only to a healthy limb or with caution to an unhealthy limb.

**Size of tourniquet:** Arm 10 cm; leg 15 cm or wider in legs

**Extremity site: Upper arm:** mid or upper thigh

**Padding:** At least two layers of orthopedic wool

**Skin preparation:** Occlude to prevent soakage of wool

**Pressure:**

**Arm:** 50-100 mmHg above systolic BP or 200-250 mmHg.

**Thigh:** Double the systolic for BP or 250-350 mmHg.

_**Note:** Large cuffs are recommended for individuals with larger limbs, rather than increasing pressure._

**Time:** Absolute maximum 3 hours, but try not to exceed 2 hours.

If the duration is greater than 2.5 hours, deflate the cuff for 10 minutes at this point.

Thereafter, deflate the cuff for 10 minutes every hour.

**Documentation:** Duration and pressure

**Calibration:** At least weekly against a mercury monitor or teet gauge.

**Maintenance:** Every three months
