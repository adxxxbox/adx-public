### Loss and Return of Function with Local Anesthetics

**Sequence of Loss**Sympathetic block resulting with peripheral vasodilation and increased skin temperature  
(**increased blood flow to cutaneous tissues)**  
     **↓**  
Loss of pain and temperature sensation  
     **↓**  
Inability to feel sharp pain-needle prick  
     **↓**  
Loss of proprioception (unable to tell where affected limbs are)  
     **↓**  
Loss of touch and pressure sensation  
     **↓**  
Motor Paralysis

  

**Sequence of Return (opposite)**  
Motor Paralysis  
     **↓**  
Loss of touch and pressure sensation  
     **↓**  
Loss of proprioception  
(unable to tell where affected limbs are)  
     **↓**  
Inability to feel sharp pain-needle prick  
     **↓**  
Loss of pain and temperature sensation  
     **↓**  
Sympathetic block resulting with peripheral vasodilation and increased skin temperature  
(**increased blood flow to cutaneous tissues)**

Chestnut, David.(2014) Chestnut’s Obstetric Anesthesia Principles and Practice.
