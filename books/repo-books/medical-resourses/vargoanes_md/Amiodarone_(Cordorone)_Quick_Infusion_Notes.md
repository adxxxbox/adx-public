# Amiodorone (Cordarone) Quick Infusion Notes

  

**Amiodorone (Cordarone)**

For ventricular arrhythmias, VF/VT, or too many PVCs

  
**VF/pulseless VT**

<table border="1" cellpadding="5" cellspacing="0"><tbody><tr><td valign="top"><p>300 mg bolus (50mg/cc from vials)<br>May repeat with 150mg x 1</p></td></tr></tbody></table>

**Wide complex tachycardia STABLE (or too many PVCs)**

<table border="1" cellpadding="5" cellspacing="0"><tbody><tr><td valign="top"><p>150 mg over the FIRST -10 minutes (15 mg/min)<b>.</b></p><p>Add 3 mL of Cordarone I.V. (150 mg) to 100 mL D <sub>5 </sub>W. Infuse 100 mL over 10 minutes. (Though not recommended, many just draw up in a syringe and slow IV push)</p></td></tr></tbody></table>

  
**THEN**

<table border="1" cellpadding="5" cellspacing="0"><tbody><tr><td valign="top"><p><b>Followed by Slow: 360 mg over the NEXT 6 hours (1 mg/min)<br></b>Can use 900mg/500ml (1.8mg/ml) bottle- Set pump- 33 ml/hr (1mg/min) x 6 hours</p></td></tr></tbody></table>

  
**THEN** (after the 6 hours)

<table border="1" cellpadding="5" cellspacing="0"><tbody><tr><td valign="top"><p><b><br>Maintenance infusion</b>: <b>540 mg over the REMAINING </b><b>18 hours (0.5 mg/min)</b></p><p><b>so just </b>decrease your pump to 17 ml/hr (30 mg/hr) for the next 18 hours</p></td></tr></tbody></table>
