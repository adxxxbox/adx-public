# Racemic Epinephrine

**Racemic Epinephrine**  
An isomer (racemic mixture) of epinephrine we give in an aerosolized form to reduce airway swelling (stridor).  
It is a diluted form of Epi.  
Prepared for aerosolization is equivalent to a 1:100 dose of epinephrine.  
**Dosage**  
**Adults:** 0.5-0.75 ml of a 2.25% solution in 2.0 ml normal saline.  
**Peds:** 0.25-0.75 ml of a 2.25% solution in 2.0 ml normal saline.  
**Indications**  
Croup (laryngotracheobronchitis)  
Post extubation stridor  
**MOA**  
In the airways, it cause mucosal vasoconstriction (tightening of the mucosa) and decreases fluid in the airway (subglottic edema) from its Alpha adrenergic properties.  
It bronchodilates with its Beta adrenergic properties the same way Ventolin (Proventil) works.  
Racemic epinephrine resulted in a significant improvement in wheezing compared to Ventolin (Proventil).  
**Side Effects**  
Increases HR by an average of 21 BPM after 60 minutes after the treatment.  
Also: nausea, anxiety, heart palpitations and headache.  
**Contraindications:**  
Cases of epiglottitis  
Hypersensitivity to the drug.
