### Temporal Artery Biopsy

To diagnose Temporal Arteritis (giant cell arteritis).

A granulomatous arteritis of unknown etiology.  
Mostly seen with > 50 year olds.  
Effects branches of the external carotid artery and the vertebral, ophthalmic, and posterior ciliary arteries.  
**Symptoms:** Headaches, jaw claudication and loss of vision.

If not early diagnosed, it can lead to permanent blindness.

**Anesthetic:** MAC  
**Duration:** 15-45 minutes  
**EBL**: None

**IV:** 22G is acceptable  
**Anesthetic considerations:** Tape the one side of the NC away from the temporal area. Head turned to opposite direction of surgical side. Sometimes the surgeon may want the bed rotated. Face is covered with a drape.  
Easy MAC case, local injection is minimal.
