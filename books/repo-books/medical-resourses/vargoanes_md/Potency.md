### Local Anesthetics & Potency

The higher the lipid solubility, the more potent the drug.  
The more potent the drug, the smaller the amount required to produce a given effect.  
Let’s look at Bupivacaine. It is a highly lipid soluble local anesthetic.  
Bupivacaine is four times more potent than Lidocaine.  
This is why Bupivacaine is pharmaceutically prepared in lower concentrations as compared to Lidocaine.  
Bupivacaine being more potent is prepared as a 0.1 – 0.75% solution. Lidocaine conversely is commonly presented as a 1% or 2% solution.  
  
Bupivacaine is commonly infused at just 0.125% for a laboring woman.  
  
**1 = least  
4 = greatest
