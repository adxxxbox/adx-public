### Dibucaine (Nupercainal) OTC

Amide-type local anesthetic.  
Known for “Dibucaine Number” used to differentiate individuals who have substitution mutations of the plasma cholinesterase (butyrylcholinesterase) enzyme resulting in decreased enzyme function.  
  
Topical Anesthetic (ointment) - **Topical and Rectal**  
Fast, temporary relief of pain and itching due to:  
Hemorrhoids  
Minor burns  
Insect bites  
Sunburn  
Other minor skin irritants.  
  
Hemorrhoids: May be used on the rectum after each bowel movement or up to 4 times per day to treat hemorrhoid pain and itching.  
  
**Doses:** Apply to affected areas; <30 g for adults or <7.5 g for children in 24-hour.  
  
Avoid getting this medication in your mouth or eyes.  
Do not use dibucaine topical on deep skin wounds or severe burns.
