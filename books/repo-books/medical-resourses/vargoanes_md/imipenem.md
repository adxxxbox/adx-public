# Imipenem

**Primaxin (Imipenem + Cilastatin)**  
**Class:** Carbapenem antibiotic (the first one)  
Broad spectrum antibiotic  
A beta-lactam  
Avoid with PCN or beta-lactam allergies.  
Penicillins and Cephalosporins are Beta-Lactams.  
For both Gram+ and Gram- bacteria.  
**Doses Range:** 250mg -1gram IV  
**Peds:**  
4weeks: 3 months:100mg/kg/day in divided doses Q6h  
Infants > 3 months and children: 60-100mg/kg/day IV  
in divided doses Q6h.  
**Max:** 4 grams/day  
When Imipenem is given alone, it is rapidly destroyed by the renal enzyme dehydropeptidase, making it less effective and the metabolites can cause kidney damage. So it is combined with Cilastatin, an inhibitor of the enzyme dehydropeptidase.  
**MOA**  
Inhibition of cell wall synthesis.  
**Side Effects**  
GI upset and diarrhea  
Nausea  
Seizures  
Headache  
Rash and allergic reactions
