### Drips Cheat Sheet

Adenosine: (Adenocard®)

**Initial bolus:** 6mg rapid bolus followed immediately by a saline flush syringe.

**May repeat:** 6mg or 12 mg bolus followed by a saline flush syringe (max 30 mg total).

Adenosine: (Adenocard®)

Phosphodiesterase inhibitor

**Loading dose:** 0.75/mg/kg

**Maintenance dose:** 5-10 mcg/kg/minute

Amrinone may cause thrombocytopenia, so it is not as frequently used as its sister drug, milrinone.

Nicardipine (Cardene®) – 10 mL vial (25 mg) or 2.5 mg/mL

Dihydropyridine calcium channel blocker

Vasodilator- selective arteriolar smooth muscle relaxation.

An arterial selective arteriolar dilator decreases SVR.

Increasingly used in preference to nitroglycerine.

5-15 mg/hour or

0.08 to 0.25 mg/min

Using a 250 mL bag of NS to get 100 mcg/mL (0.1 mg/mL) of Cardene: Standard

-   Aspirate and waste 10 mLs from the 250 mL bag of NS
-   add 25 mg (10 mLs) of Cardene to the 240 mL bag- yields 100 mcg/mL (0.1 mg/mL)

Using a 250 mL bag of NS to get 200 mcg/mL (0.2 mg/mL) of Cardene):

Use two (25 mg) vials (20 mLs total) of Cardene

-   Aspirate and waste 20 mLs from the 250 mL bag of NS
-   add 50 mg (20 mLs) of Cardene to the 230 mL bag- yields 200 mcg/mL (0.2 mg/mL)

Diltiazem (Cardizem®)

Dihydropyridine calcium channel blocker

**Rapid A-fib:** 20 mg bolus (undiluted) then 5-15 mg/hour (1mg/mL mixture is common)

**Atrial flutter:** 20 mg bolus (undiluted) over 2 minutes.

**If no conversion:** 25mg or (0.35mg/kg) 15 minutes after the initial 20mg

**PSVT:** 20 mg or (0.25 mg/kg) slow push over 2 minutes.

**If PSVT does not convert to sinus rhythm:** 25mg or (0.35mg/kg) 15 minutes after the initial 20mg

100 mg in a 100 mL bag yields 1mg/mL (1,000 mcg/mL)

Clevidipine (Cleviprex®)

Dihydropyridine calcium channel blocker

Milky-white lipid emulsion in single-use glass vials

Vasodilator-- selective arteriolar smooth muscle relaxation.

A selective arteriolar dilator decreases SVR.

Faster onset and shorter duration than Cardene

Contains 2 grams of lipids per ml.

1 to 16 mg/hour

or

0.02 to 0.27 mg/minute

Fenoldopam (Corlopam®)

No longer available in the US.

Rapid-acting vasodilator

**Dose:** Initial 0.1 mcg/kg/min

**Average range:** 0.25 to 0.5 mcg/kg/min or

1 to 3 mcg/kg/hour

Increases urine output while decreasing BP.

Dobutamine (Dobutrex®)

Inotrope, vasodilator (decreases SVR)

Beta1 and beta2 adrenergic receptor agonist

Predominant beta1 adrenergic receptor effect increases inotropy and chronotropy and reduces left ventricular filling pressure.

**Net effect:** Increased CO, decreased SVR with or without a slight reduction in BP.

**Dose:** 1 to 20 mcg/kg/minute

Dopamine (Intropin®)

**Class:** inotrope/vasopressor

An immediate precursor to norepinephrine. Characterized by dose-dependent effects on specific receptors.

Activates dopaminergic, alpha, and beta-adrenergic receptors.

**Low-dose:** 1 to 5 mcg/kg/minute (renal); increases renal blood flow and urine output.

Acts predominantly on dopamine-1 receptors in the renal, mesenteric, cerebral, and coronary beds, resulting in selective vasodilation.

**Intermediate-dose:** 5 to 10 mcg/kg/minute (cardiac); increases renal blood flow, heart rate, cardiac contractility, stroke volume, and cardiac output.

Has a greater effect on cardiac beta1 than on alpha receptors.

**High-dose:**10 to 20 mcg/kg/minute (pressor); alpha-adrenergic effects begin to predominate, resulting in vasoconstriction (increased SVR), increased blood pressure, in addition to increased heart rate, cardiac contractility, and cardiac output.

Alpha greater than beta.

**Max dose:** 50 mcg/kg/minute

Ephedrine

**Class:** vasopressor; non-catecholamine sympathomimetic

Direct and indirect actions.

**Direct acting:** On alpha, beta adrenergic agonist.

**Indirect acting:** Causes a release of norepinephrine and other endogenous catecholamines stored within nerve terminals.

5-20 mg bolus range.

100-200 mcg/kg Q 5-10 minutes as needed.

Epinephrine

1:1,000 = 1mg/ml (small 1 ml vials or ampules)

1:10,000 = 0.1 mg (100 mcg)/ml

**Class:** Sympathomimetic, vasopressor

Direct alpha- and beta-adrenergic receptor agonist

**Receptor activity (greatest to least):** Beta2 > Beta1 > Alpha2 > Alpha1

Dilution for small boluses:

Add one 1mg vial of epinephrine (1:1,000) to a 250 ml bag of NS = 4 mcg/ml

Add two 1 mg vials of epinephrine to a 250 mL bag of NS = 8 mcg/mL

Add four 1 mg vials of epinephrine to a 250 mL bag of NS = 16 mcg/mL (double concentrated)

Infusions:

2 to 10 mcg/minute (beta1 adrenergic, beta adrenergic)

10 to 100 mcg/minute (alpha adrenergic)

It can be titrated up by 1-2 mcg/minute.

Esmolol (Brevibloc®)

Adrenergic antagonist and anti-arrhythmic

Selective beta1 adrenergic receptor antagonist

Fast onset with short duration.

**Initial bolus:** 0.5 mg/kg over 1 minute.

**Infusion:** 10 to 50 mg boluses, then 25 to 300 mcg/kg/minute.

**Common starting infusion dose:** 50 mcg/kg/min

Isoproterenol (Isuprel®)

Inotrope/chronotropic/vasodilator

Beta1 and beta2 adrenergic receptor agonist

Causes vasodilation and a decrease in MAP

5 to 20 mcg/minute

or

0.05 to 0.2 mcg/kg/minute

Mixtures:

0.2 mg in a 50 mL bag yields 4 mcg/mL

0.4 mg in a 100 mL bag yields 4 mcg/mL

1.0 mg in a 250 mL bag yields 4 mcg/m

Labetalol (Trandate®)

Adrenergic antagonist

Nonselective beta1, beta2, and alpha1adrenergic receptor antagonist

5 to 20 mg IV boluses, then 0.5 to 2 mg/minute

Metoprolol (Lopressor®)

Adrenergic antagonist and antiarrhythmic

Selective beta1 adrenergic receptor antagonist

Common beta blocker used perioperatively for CAD patients.

Slower onset and longer duration than esmolol.

Consider starting with a lower dose due to its duration.

**Initial bolus:** 1 to 5 mg for reducing heart rate; up to a maximum of 15 mg.

Consider starting with a lower dose due to its duration.

**Initial bolus:** 1 to 5 mg for reducing heart rate up to a maximum of 15 mg.

Milrinone (Primacor®)

Inotrope/vasodilator

Phosphodiesterase 3 inhibitor (decreases degradation of cyclic adenosine monophosphate \[cAMP\])

Increases contractility and cardiac index

Decreases capillary wedge pressure

Potent pulmonary vasodilator (decreases PAP)

**Loading dose** 50 mcg/kg over 10 or more minutes (usually skipped due to hypotension), then

**Maintenance dose:** 0.375 to 0.75 mcg/kg/minute.

Nitroglycerin (Tridil®)

Vasodilators (primarily a venous dilator, an arteriolar dilator to a lesser degree).

Increase in cyclic guanosine monophosphate (cGMP)

Often manually titrated to effect.

10 to 400 mcg/minute

or

to 4 mcg/kg/minute

Nitroprusside (Nipride®)

Vasodilator (venous and arteriolar)

Smooth muscle relaxation

Doses >400 mcg/minute are not recommended due to minimal added benefit and increased risk for thiocyanate or cyanide toxicity.

10 to 400 mcg/minute

or

0.1 to 4 mcg/kg/minute, max 10mcg/kg/minute

Cannot mix in NS. D5W only.

Norepinephrine (Levophed®)

Class: Symptomatic; alpha1, alpha2, and beta1 adrenergic receptor agonist.

Doses of norepinephrine

1-30 mcg/min (2-4 most common) or

0.5-2 mcg/kg/min for refractory CHF or

0.1-0.5 for ACLS

0.01 to 0.3 mcg/kg/minute

4mg (one 4 mL vial) in a 250 mL bag of NS yields 16 mcg/mL (standard)

8mg (two 4 mL vials) in a 250 mL bag of NS yields 32 mcg/mL

Phenylephrine (Neosynephrine®)

Alpha adrenergic agonist

**Manual infusion:** Titrate to effect

**Infusion rates:** 10 to 200 mcg/minute or 0.1 to 2 mcg/kg/minute

Mixtures:

10 mg (one 1mL vial) in a 250 mL bag of NS yields 40 mcg/mL (standard)

20 mg (two 1mL vials) in a 250 mL bag of NS yields 80 mcg/mL

40 mg (four 1 mL vials) in a 250 mL bag of NS yields 160 mcg/mL

5 mg (1/2 of one 1 mL vial) in a 100 mLs bag of NS yields 50 mcg/mL

10 mg (one 1mL vial) in a 100 mL bag of NS yields 100 mcg/mL

10 mg (one 1mL vial) in a 500 mL bag of NS yields 20 mcg/mL

Procainamide (Pronestyl®)

Antiarrhythmic

2 grams / 250 ml

**Usual range:** 1 – 4 mg / min

1mg/min = 7.5 ml/hr

2 mg/min = 15 ml/hr

3 mg/min = 22.5 ml/hr

4 mg/min = 30 ml/hr

1,000 mg in 250 mL bag of NS yields 4 mg/mL

Vasopressin (Pitressin®, ADH)

Vasopressin1 and vasopressin2 receptor agonist

Common doses of vasopressin for hypotension

Rescue pressor of choice for pulmonary hypertension

1-2 units IV push for intraoperative refractory hypotension.

Septic shock: 0.01 to 0.2 units/min (concomitantly with Levophed)

Bleeding esophageal varices: 0.2 to 0.4 units (max 0.8 units per min)

Mixtures:

20 units (one 20 unit 1 mL vial) added to 9 mLs in a 10 mL syringe yields 2 units/mL

20 units (one 20 unit 1 mL vial) added to 19 mLs in a 20 mL syringe yields 1 units/mL

20 units (one 20 unit 1 mL vial) added to a 50 mL NS bag yields 0.4 units/mL

40 units (two 20 unit vials) added to a 100 mL bag yields 0.4 units/mL

100 units (five 20 unit vials) added to a 500 mL bag yields 0.2 units/mL

### Narcotics/Analgesics

Lidocaine (Xylocaine®)

**For ventricular arrhythmias:**1-1.5 mg/kg IV bolus PRN. Max total of 2 mg/kg.

**Perioperative infusion for pain:** 0.5 to 3 mg/kg/hour (after a bolus of 0 to 1.5 mg/kg)

1 gram (1,000 mg) injected into 250 ml NS yields 4 mg/mL

2 grams (2,000 mg) injected into 500 ml normal saline yields 4 mg/ml

Dexmedetomidine (Precedex®)

Selective alpha2-adrenoceptor agonist

**Common mixture:** 4 mcg/ml

**Loading dose:** 0.5 mg/kg over 30 minutes

**Infusion and sedation:** 0.2 to 1.0 mcg/kg/hour

**Peds smooth emergence:** Dilute 25 mcg in a

5 ml syringe (5 mcg/ml), manually titrate to effect.

Aspirate and waste 2 mLs from a 50 mL of NS bag, then add 200 mcg (one 2 mL vial) of Precedex, yielding 4 mcg/mL

Aspirate and waste 4 mLs from a 100 mL of NS bag, then add 400 mcg (two 2 mL vials) of Precedex yielding 4 mcg/mL

Add 200 mcg (one 2 mL vial) of Precedex to 38 mLs of NS (40 cc syringe) yields 10 mcg/mL

Isopropylphenol (Propofol®)

**Induction dose:** 1-2.5 mcg/kg

**Sedation:** 25-100 mcg/kg/minute

**TIVA:** 100 to 300 mcg/kg/minute

Remifentanil (Ultiva®)

Ultra-short-acting synthetic opioid

Dose should be calculated based on ideal body weight

**Infusion range:** 0.025 to 0.2 mcg/kg/minute

**Intubation doses:** 0.5-1 mcg/kg/minute IV until after intubation.

**Anesthesia maintenance (TIVA):** 0.20-0.5 mcg/kg/minute IV.

**Initial infusion rate:** 0.15 to 0.2 mcg/kg/minute.

0.2mcg/kg/minute is a dose commonly used to infuse concomitantly with a propofol infusion with no MAC or ½ MAC of inhalation agent.

**Bolus for increased surgical pain or light anesthesia:** 0.5-1 mcg/kg q2-5 minutes.

**MAC infusion rate:**1 mcg/kg IV bolus, followed by 0.05-0.2 mcg/kg/min IV

1 mg (powder) in 20 ml of NS syringe yields 50 mcg/ml.

2 mg (powder) in 40 ml of NS syringe yields 50 mcg/ml.

Aspirate and waste 10 mLs from a 50 mL bag, add 2 mg to the 40 mL bag, yields 50 mcg/mL= (2,000/40 mLs)

Sufentanil (Sufenta®)

Synthetic opioid

5 to 10 times more potent than fentanyl.

Dose should be calculated based on ideal body weight.

**Common induction dose:** 0.1-0.3 mcg/kg

**Common PRN bolus for pain:** 0.1 mcg/kg or 5-10 mcg bolus.

**Common infusion:** 0.2 mcg/kg/hour

**Infusion range:** 0.2-0.5 mcg/kg/hour

Vial: 50 mcg/mL

4 ml (200 mcg) of Sufenta added to a 16 ml saline syringe yields 10 mcg/ml

10 ml (500 mcg) of Sufenta added to 40 ml saline (using a 50 mL syringe) yields 10 mcg/ml

Aspirate and waste 10 mLs from a 50 mL bag, add 10 mL (500 mcg) to the 40 mL bag, yields 10 mcg/mL (500/50 mLs)
