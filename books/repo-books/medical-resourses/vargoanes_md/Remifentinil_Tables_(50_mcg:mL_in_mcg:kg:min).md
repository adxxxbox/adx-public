# Remifentanil (Ultiva)

**TIVA**  
2mg in 40mL = 50mcg/mL in a syringe  
1mg in 20mL = 50mcg/mL in a syringe  
**Range:** 0.2 - 2mcg/kg/min (independently-no other drips)  
**Range:** 0.025-0.25mcg/kg/min (adjunct to Propofol)-less required.  
Many CRNAs just start at 0.2mcg/kg/min and titrate down with a slower heart rate.  
I have seen CRNAs start at 0.1mcg/kg/min and just leave it at that dose.  
Serum 1/2 life is 3-6 minutes  
Stop infusion 5-10 minutes prior to emergence.  
Consider a longer acting narcotic while the Remi drip is turned off.  

**MAC (Remi and Propofol)**  
Start Remi at 0.1 mcg/kg/min x 3 mins then drop it to .05/mcg/kg/min. You can titrate changes of .01 to keep RR around 12bpm. At the same time, on a different pump, run Propofol at 50mcg/kg/min. Do not dare bolus even 1cc of Propofol while running Remi because the patient may go apneic. (M. Antoszyk CRNA).  

**Dosing regimens should be based on ideal body weight or lean body mass and NOT total body weight**  
**Range:**0.025-2mcg/kg/min  
2mg in 40mL = 50mcg/mL in syringe  
1mg in 20mL = 50mcg/mL in syringe **Equation:**  
Desired dose x kg x 60 / mcg/mL = mL per hr infusion rate

* * *

**Example:**  
You desire 0.2mcg/kg/min on a 70kg patient with a standard 50mcg/mL concentration.  
0.2 x 70 x 60 / 50 =  
\= 0.2 x 4,200 /50 mL/hr  
\= 840/50 = 16.8mLs/hour
