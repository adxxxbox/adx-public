# Zosyn

**Zosyn (Piperacillin + Tazobactam)**  
Piperacillin is Penicillin.  
Tazobactam inhibits the action of bacterial beta-lactamases.  
Tazobactam broadens the spectrum of Piperacillin by making it effective against organisms that express beta-lactamase and would normally degrade piperacillin.  
**Dose:**  
Each 3.375 grams of Zosyn contains 3 grams of Piperacillin and 0.375 grams of Tazobactam.  
Also contains 0.75 mg of EDTA per vial.  
Infuse over 30 minutes  
Don't administer Zosyn if the patient is allergic to Piperacillin or Tazobactam or to any other penicillin antibiotic, such as:  
amoxicillin (Amoxil, Augmentin, Dispermox, Moxatag);  
ampicillin (Principen, Unasyn);  
dicloxacillin (Dycill, Dynapen);  
oxacillin (Bactocill);  
ticarcillin (Timentin); or  
penicillin (Bicillin L-A, PC Pen VK, Pfizerpen), and others.  
FDA pregnancy category B. Zosyn is not expected to be harmful to an unborn baby.  
**Preparation:** Also come in vials of 2.25g and 4.5 grams. I have never seen one.  
**Possible Side Effects:**  
Diarrhea that is watery or bloody.  
Jaundice  
Dark colored urine  
Fever, confusion or weakness  
SOB or Tachycardia  
Easy bruising, unusual bleeding (nose, mouth, vagina, or rectum) Petechiae
